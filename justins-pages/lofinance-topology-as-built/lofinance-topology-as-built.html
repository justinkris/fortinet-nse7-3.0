Redistribution

All redistribution defaults to disabled. Exactly one static is redistributed at the hub: the DR legacy management subnet 10.99.50.0/24, gated by access-list MGMT-ONLY with exact-match enable. Automatic trust = blast radius; the filter is the leash.

  • Fingerprint to remember: Full/ - everywhere on the tunnels — no DR/BDR elections run on point-to-multipoint. A role column with content means an election ran somewhere it shouldn't have.
  • Backbone rule: every non-backbone area must connect to Area 0; DR2PAR exists partly so a HQ failure cannot partition the backbone.
  • Symmetry is your job, not OSPF's: cost is paid on exit; asymmetric costs across parallel paths break stateful firewalls (S16's Asymmetric Cost Trap).

09 · How It Got Here

Topology Evolution, Session by Session

S1 — Baseline

Sydney HQ only: Internet → edge FortiGate → core switch → server room, trading floor, exec floor, DMZ, management network, guest Wi-Fi.

S4 — Segmentation

VLAN 5 (exec) and VLAN 20 (trading, 400 desks) on dot1q trunk internal1; gateways on the FortiGate; deny-by-default between them.

S5 — Correction

Policy dstintf fixed from physical trunk to VLAN sub-interface; PrintGroup software-switch bundle documented as intentional.

S6–S7 — VDOMs

root, Trading (isolated) and Guest-WiFi VDOMs; inter-VDOM link VDOM_Link0/1 gives guests internet with zero path to Trading. Junior's $340 switch cancelled.

S8 — The OpsBridge Purge

OpsBridge switch-interface deleted; VLAN 10 (Finance) and VLAN 30 (Support) re-isolated via policy 12; Guest-WiFi session override → 0.

S10–S11 — Parramatta Designed

Hub-and-spoke chosen against the actual traffic pattern; both HQ↔Parramatta tunnels specified IKEv2 with network-id 1 and 2. Designed, not built.

S12 — First Spoke Live

Parramatta FortiGate deployed; ParraTunnel-ISP1 in production carrying trading-feed access; ISP2 configured but dark. Subnets canonised.

S13 — Redundancy Real

Parramatta wan2 live; ParraTunnel-ISP2 up; ParraAgg aggregate (redundant) built both ends; routes and policies migrated to it. Chatswood lease signed.

S14 — The Fleet Named

47 static routes counted across HQ, DR, Parramatta and the boxed Chatswood unit — the DR site enters the record; OSPF planned, Chatswood as Area 1.

S15 — Chatswood Live

Area 1 dial-up spoke; hub interface fixed to point-to-multipoint; hub becomes ABR + ASBR + backbone router; 10.99.50.0/24 redistributed, filtered.

S17 — DR2PAR Proven

DR↔Parramatta backup tunnel verified end-to-end under a three-fault cowboy-hat drill — a genuine second Area-0 path.

S20–S21 — The SSL Crusade

Certificate inspection, then full deep inspection outbound with exemptions and CA distribution; inbound portal protection; TLS 1.0 confined to one expiring doorway.

S22 — Filters

Web filtering and application control layered on. Topology grew smarter, not bigger.

S23 — Inward Ratings

FortiManager promoted to local FDS; fleet rates inward; public anycast fallback disabled; renewal signed.

S24 — The Tripwire

ISFW promoted to NGFW dual-role; IPS sensor protect-trading-api attached to the guest-VLAN→trading boundary — in Monitor. Strung, not yet armed.
