Title: Riley's Ticket — The Seam Between Sorters

Purpose: Encode the exact ticket Riley files at the trading floor —
"Facebook blocked but the Facebook APP still works." The Web Filter
reads the ADDRESS on browser traffic and stops it, but the mobile
app's envelope carries no address the Web Filter recognises the
same way. Only the App Control station, one step further down the
belt, can catch it by its HANDWRITING. Viewer should immediately
recall: one symptom, two sorters, and knowing which one sees what
is the whole ticket.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration,
storybook-professional style, cream/warm off-white background
(#faf5e9), coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft
brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no
photorealism, no 3D. Horizontal composition (16:9), interior scene
of the same warm LoFinance mail-sorting hall from the master
illustration. In the FOREGROUND at the very front of frame: a
crisp help-desk ticket printed on cream paper, resting on a small
brass ticket-holder on the belt's edge, with a handwritten heading
"HELP-DESK TICKET #57" in soft brown, subheading
"trading-floor@lofinance" and body text (visible but stylised, not
paragraph-heavy) reading: "Facebook is blocked but the Facebook
APP still works?? — Trading Floor." A small coral highlighter
mark crosses the word "APP". A tiny LoFinance frozen-money logo
(stacks of banknotes inside an ice cube) sits in the ticket's top
corner. The ticket casts a subtle soft shadow to suggest depth.

BEHIND the ticket, the conveyor belt runs left to right with TWO
stations clearly visible and one background station faded:

STATION 1 (LEFT, coral canopy) — "WEB FILTER". The clerk with
reading glasses (same character as the master illustration) holds
up a BROWSER envelope — a cream envelope with a small stylised
browser-window motif drawn on its face (address bar, three dots,
a URL "facebook.com" written across the address bar in soft brown
script). She reads the URL through a magnifying glass, points at
the wall directory behind her ("FortiGuard Categories") where
"Social Networking — BLOCK" is stamped red, and firmly hands the
envelope back with a stop-gesture. A small red rubber stamp
"RETURNED" hovers above the envelope. Below her feet, a caption
ribbon reads "reads the ADDRESS".

STATION 2 (CENTRE, sage canopy) — "APP CONTROL". The handwriting
expert (same character as the master, with wire spectacles and
open reference book) is BUSY examining a completely DIFFERENT
envelope — this second envelope is APP-SHAPED: a rounded phone-app
tile shape, coral-blue tinted, decorated with a small stylised
mobile-app icon on its face (a rounded square with a chat-bubble
motif — NOT any real logo). This app envelope has NO address bar,
NO URL visible on it — just faint loops of ink meant to be
"handwriting" (curly signature lines drawn in coral). The expert
squints at those loops through his own magnifying glass, thought
bubble showing "hmm… familiar loops…" — but the app envelope has
already crossed past Station 1 without being touched. Visible
motion lines suggest it slipped through the gap between stations.
Below him, a caption ribbon reads "recognises the HANDWRITING".

The seam between Station 1 and Station 2 is highlighted subtly:
draw a faint dashed gold line vertically down between the two
canopies, with a tiny label at the top reading "THE SEAM" in
small caps — this is the memory anchor of the image.

STATION 3 (BACKGROUND RIGHT, gold canopy, deliberately faded and
smaller for depth) — "ISDB": the unopened-mail postcode-grid
sorter from the master illustration, present but softly muted so
it does not compete with the two front stations. This shows the
belt continues but keeps the focus on stations 1 and 2.

Small figure of Riley (help-desk lead, professional but slightly
harried, wearing a phone headset, holding a clipboard with more
ticket printouts) stands to the extreme left of the frame gesturing
at the ticket in the foreground — she is the one who filed it.
Small figure of Junior in the mid-background looks confused,
scratching his head, holding the app envelope's twin in his hand
and looking between the two stations. Sombrero-wearing Señor
stands calmly on a mezzanine behind Station 2, arms folded, with
a quiet knowing expression — he is NOT pointing or fixing
anything; he is letting the viewer see the seam for themselves.

Visual hierarchy: (1) ticket in foreground is brightest and most
detailed; (2) the two envelopes — browser at station 1, app at
station 2 — are the second focal point; (3) the dashed gold seam
line between the canopies is the third; (4) characters and
background stations are supporting detail.

Mood: instructive, slightly wry, unmistakably a "one symptom, two
sorters" teaching frame. It should read as calm competence, not
alarm.

MUST include: the ticket text visible and legible; two visibly
DIFFERENT envelope shapes (browser rectangle with address bar vs
app tile with icon); the dashed "THE SEAM" line between the two
canopies; caption ribbons "reads the ADDRESS" and "recognises the
HANDWRITING".

MUST NOT include: any real Facebook logo, real Meta branding,
real vendor UI screenshots, gradients, glossy 3D, photorealism,
dense CLI text, alarm bells or red X marks (this is a calm
teaching illustration, not a dramatic error).

Aspect ratio: 16:9
