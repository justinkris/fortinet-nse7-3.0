Title: The LoFinance Mailroom — Five Inspectors, One Open Letter

Aspect Ratio · 16:9

Purpose: Anchor the whole mental model in a single memorable scene — four inspectors examining the outside of an envelope while only IPS opens it and reads the intent inside.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed illustrated artwork, modern vector-art aesthetic, on a warm cream off-white background with no gradients and no photorealism. A cross-section view of a busy corporate mailroom shown as a left-to-right conveyor belt carrying one large cream envelope stamped with the LoFinance logo (a stack of money frozen inside an ice cube). Five distinct inspector characters stand along the belt, each in a small labelled station, drawn with clean bold soft-brown outlines and consistent storybook proportions. Station 1, "Firewall": a stern uniformed guard at a turnstile gate lifting a boom barrier, checking whether the envelope may enter — muted gold accents. Station 2, "Web Filter": a clerk holding a magnifying glass over the ADDRESS written on the front of the envelope — sage green apron. Station 3, "App Control": a clerk squinting at the HANDWRITING style on the envelope, a handwriting-sample card in hand — soft blue. Station 4, "ISDB": a clerk pointing at a wall map of city suburbs, matching the address to a neighbourhood — muted gold. Station 5 sits at the far right, larger and highlighted in coral: "IPS" — a focused inspector who has actually TORN THE ENVELOPE OPEN and is reading the letter inside, holding it beside a reference book titled "Known Attack Signatures"; a small coral warning glow radiates from the opened letter. Above each of the first four stations, a small speech bubble asks about the OUTSIDE of the envelope (who / where / how); above IPS a coral speech bubble asks "What is it trying to DO?". Clear visual hierarchy makes the IPS station the focal point. Generous spacing, clean line work, professional but storybook-like, readable as an educational diagram. Coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines. Do NOT include: photorealistic faces, glossy 3D rendering, dense technical jargon labels, real brand logos other than the described LoFinance ice-cube mark, gore or weapons.

Accessibility: A mailroom conveyor belt with five inspector stations examining one envelope; the first four check the outside (gate, address, handwriting, suburb map) while the coral-highlighted IPS inspector opens the letter and reads its contents against an attack-signature book.

Memory Anchor: IPS is the only inspector who opens the letter — it inspects intent, not the envelope.

Title: The Checkpoint — Ticket, Destination, Airline… then the X-Ray

Aspect Ratio · 16:9

Purpose: Reinforce the same layered-inspection model with a higher-stakes metaphor, and make the "signature match" idea concrete through the luggage X-ray reading against a known-threat reference.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed illustrated artwork, modern vector-art aesthetic, warm cream off-white background, no gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D. A single traveller — a friendly, slightly nervous cartoon passenger carrying one wheeled suitcase stamped with the LoFinance ice-cube logo — moves left to right through four airport security stages, drawn with clean bold soft-brown outlines and consistent storybook proportions. Stage 1, "Firewall": a gate agent scanning a boarding pass at a turnstile, muted gold accents — caption bubble "Ticket?". Stage 2, "Web Filter": a departures board where an agent points at the traveller's DESTINATION city, sage green accents — caption bubble "Where to?". Stage 3, "App Control": an agent checking which AIRLINE logo is on the ticket, soft blue accents — caption bubble "Which airline?". Stage 4, highlighted and larger in coral, "IPS": a luggage X-ray belt where the suitcase is OPEN and a security officer studies the X-ray screen; the screen clearly shows a suspicious silhouette outlined and pulsing coral, and beside the monitor sits an open reference binder labelled "Known Threat Patterns / Signatures" with matching silhouettes — the officer is pointing from screen to binder to show the MATCH. A coral caption bubble over IPS reads "What are you carrying?". Strong visual hierarchy places the X-ray station as the focal point. The first three stages examine the passenger's OUTSIDE credentials; only IPS inspects the CONTENTS. Generous spacing, clean readable educational-diagram style, coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines. Do NOT include: real weapons drawn in graphic detail (keep any flagged item as a neutral abstract coral silhouette, not a realistic weapon), gore, photorealistic faces, real airline or airport brand logos, dense technical text.

Accessibility: A passenger passing four airport checks — ticket, destination, airline, then an X-ray. Only the coral-highlighted IPS X-ray station opens the luggage and matches a suspicious silhouette on screen against a binder of known threat patterns.

Memory Anchor: IPS is the X-ray: it opens the bag and matches contents against known-threat signatures.
