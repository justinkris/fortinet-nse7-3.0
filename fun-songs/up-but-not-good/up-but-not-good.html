Cheatsheet — Hook Line ↔ Concept Tap to open
Line from the songWhat it encodesWhere it lives
"Up, but not good" Reachability ≠ health. UP means the link is alive. It says nothing about latency, jitter, or loss. SLA — the sensor
"Routing says I can get there, SD-WAN says should I though" CAN vs SHOULD. Routing answers reachability; SD-WAN answers fitness — and keeps re-answering. The two brains
"The table stays asleep — the rule's the one alive" Out-of-SLA never removes the route. It removes the member's eligibility for the rule. Rules vs routes
"A member's just a road and a ramp to get you on it" Member = interface + gateway. The gateway is what FortiGate uses to auto-generate the static route. S63 nibble
"The health check is a thermometer, it reads but doesn't act" Sensor vs actuator. SLA measures. Rule acts. They must never be confused. S62 flagged
"Failtime times interval, don't forget to multiply" Time to declare out-of-SLA. 5 × 500ms = 2.5s of consistent evidence. Recoverytime is longer on purpose. Timer math
"Quick to call it broken, slow to say goodbye" Failtime is fast, recoverytime is slow. One good reading proves nothing. Asymmetric hysteresis
"A zone's the seam between the wires and the gate" Zones are the organisational boundary between network and security teams. Add a member, no policy edit. Zone as seam
"The rule's a dispatcher working from a shortlist every time" Decision at config time; answer at packet time. Every packet re-tests the shortlist. Dispatcher
"Up, eligible, preferred — three gates and not one less" Three states, in order: UP (link alive) → ELIGIBLE (in rule's list AND in-SLA) → PREFERRED (wins strategy right now). Member gates
"Out at the edge, count the badges — that's the test" DIA gives every site its own public identity. Partner allowlists shatter. Eight badges instead of one. S62 flagged
