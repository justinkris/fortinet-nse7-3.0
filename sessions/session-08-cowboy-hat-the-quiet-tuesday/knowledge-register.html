GROWTH AREAS (need repeated guidance)
=========================================================================
- RECURRING PATTERN, now seen across THREE sessions (Session 4/5 dstintf-sequencing
  retrieval, Session 7 Q2 exact-failure-stage answer, Session 8 Q2 policy-evaluation
  answer): first-pass answers under direct retrieval/exam pressure are looser or even
  contradict evidence the student personally gathered minutes earlier in the same
  session, even though the underlying reasoning is clearly present and recoverable in
  one exchange. This is the single most consistent, trackable growth thread across the
  whole course so far. TECHNIQUE THAT WORKS (confirmed three times): redirect the
  student explicitly back to his own just-gathered evidence rather than re-explaining
  the concept — this reliably produces immediate, clean self-correction. Recommend
  continuing to build future retrieval questions as genuine exam-pressure moments
  (no scaffolding in the question itself) specifically to keep testing whether this
  gap is closing, and keep using the "point back at your own evidence" redirect when
  it doesn't close on the first try — do not simply re-teach the concept from scratch.
- CLI syntax recall (not command SELECTION) is meaningfully behind conceptual/
  diagnostic reasoning. Session 8 alone: `unset member "X" "Y"` (invalid syntax),
  `diagnose sys reset` (not a real command), `diagnose sys session count` (guessed;
  real command is `diagnose sys session stat`), an invalid `show config system
  vdom-property` fragment mid-edit-sequence. In every case the student correctly
  identified WHAT KIND of command he needed before guessing at its exact form. This is
  expected given limited hands-on-keyboard FortiGate time and is explicitly not a
  reasoning gap — but it is a real, trackable exam risk for any question requiring the
  student to SUPPLY exact syntax from memory rather than interpret given output.
  Recommend a dedicated light CLI-syntax-recall drill in a future session, kept
  separate from diagnostic-reasoning practice (which does not need more work).
- FortiGuard/threat-intelligence dependency thread (tracker [4]) has gone stale —
  flagged as overdue in the Session 5 notes and still not actioned three sessions
  later. Needs a retrieval touch soon or it risks being genuinely forgotten rather
  than just untested.
