   The Q7 evidence is unambiguous: he UNDERSTANDS things faster than he RETAINS them, and

   He is a strong verbal reasoner and a weak silent retriever; converting understanding into

2. ANSWERING THE SHAPE OF THE QUESTION RATHER THAN THE STATE SPECIFIED — FOURTH OCCURRENCE.
   (S58 once, S59 twice, S60 once.) Asked which DRAWER best-path runs in, he answered "lowest

   The prescribed habit ("NAME THE DRAWER BEFORE YOU DIAGNOSE") IS NOT YET INSTALLED. He used

   to take — but it does not fire when the question is unfamiliar in shape.
   ENFORCE AS A HARD GATE IN S61: refuse to accept any diagnosis that does not open by naming
   the drawer. Do not soften this; it is the fourth session.
   WHAT WORKS: reducing the question to a fill-in-the-blank with one word per gap. He got it
   instantly that way. Use that technique when he over-reaches.

3. MULTI-SELECT METHOD — DIAGNOSED, REPLACED, UNTESTED.
   Reported method: "eliminate, count what feels right." Relative, not absolute; and the count
   was functioning as a STOPPING CONDITION rather than a checksum, which means late options
   often went unread.
   PRESCRIBED REPLACEMENT (enforce from S61):
     1. Read the stem twice and WRITE DOWN THE STATE.
     2. Score EVERY option independently TRUE/FALSE against that state, not against each other.
     3. Count TRUEs LAST and compare to the number requested.
        Too few  → you rejected something for unfamiliarity, not falsity.
        Too many → one is true in general but not of THIS state. That is the one to drop.
   Compression: "SCORE FIRST, COUNT LAST." "The count is a checksum, not a stopping condition."
   NOTE THE CONVERGENCE: this method and Growth Area 2 are the same discipline in two settings —
   read the state, then evaluate against it. Teach them as one thing from S61 onward.

4. OVER-TIGHT PERMISSION CALIBRATION — THIRD OCCURRENCE, but IMPROVING.
   Failed the RR relay retest cold by assuming a permission was withheld. Recovered by himself.
   The instinct ("features are off until enabled") is correct for FortiOS defaults and should
   NOT be trained out — it is calibration, not principle, that is wrong.
   Fix: keep asking "what happens if you configure nothing?" as a standard question on every
   new feature, so that defaults become explicit knowledge rather than assumption.

5. LOCAL RIB vs ROUTING TABLE CONFLATION (minor, new). Two slips this session. He knows the
   distinction from S55. Add the fourth box to the drawer diagram.
