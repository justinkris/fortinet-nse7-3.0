Seven Firewalls, One Junior

Vaultline's estate arrives on a pallet. Junior configures by hand. The fix isn't faster typing — it's one box that makes the typing irrelevant.

The real cost of manual management isn't labour — it's drift, and drift is an audit finding.
Why central management exists

The number that hurts

Junior counts firewalls: seven. Señor counts something else: N devices × M changes. Seven boxes and twenty standard changes a year is 140 hand-edits — and each one is a chance to fat-finger a syslog IP or skip a box entirely.

The casualty isn't your afternoon. It's consistency. In a regulated financial firm, "I think the seven firewalls match" is a compliance finding, not an inconvenience.

💰
Say it money-first
"When the auditor asks me to prove all seven firewalls enforce the same standard, hand-config means I can't — that's a finding. FortiManager proves it from one source."
The architecture

Three layers, top to bottom

LayerHoldsScope
Global databaseUniversal objects/policies (syslog, NTP, DNS)Whole company — define once, assign down
Local ADOMThat tenant's policies, admins, objects, firmwareOne tenant — hard-walled from the others
Device databaseFMG's copy of one firewall's actual configOne box (7 firewalls → 7 device DBs)
ADOM = Administrative Domain. A partition inside one FortiManager with its own admins, policies, objects, and firmware version. Vaultline's 7.4 spares + 7.6 units live in a Vaultline ADOM; LoFinance stays in its own 7.6 ADOM. A Vaultline admin literally cannot see an LF- box — because it's not in their ADOM.
The vocabulary that trips people

Two arrows and a reserved letter

TermDirection / Meaning
Retrieve / ImportFirewall → FMG. Onboarding. Do this first — retrieve before you rule.
InstallFMG → firewall. Day-2 push of the master.
AssignGlobal DB → down into local ADOMs.
Firmware windowVersion X supports X, X-1, X-2 → 7.6 covers 7.6 / 7.4 / 7.2.
Prefix gReserved for global objects — hard-blocked. You cannot create a local g- object.
The two design rules

Where to draw the walls

Drawing an ADOM: around the administrative + policy unit — who owns it, who audits it, what policy package it shares. NOT the site (org chart). Firmware is a constraint that can force a split, never the primary reason.
Global vs local: "True for everyone?" → global DB. "Just this tenant?" → their ADOM. Shove everything global and you dissolve the isolation — you may as well run one big ADOM.
