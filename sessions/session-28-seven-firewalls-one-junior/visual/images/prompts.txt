Title: 1 · The FortiManager Building (hero cutaway)

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, modern storybook-technical style, on a warm cream off-white background (#faf5e9), clean bold soft-brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D. A cutaway cross-section of a three-storey building shaped like a sleek data-centre tower, viewed straight-on so all three floors are visible at once like a doll's house.

TOP FLOOR (penthouse), tinted soft blue (#eff4fc, blue border): a wide open loft labelled "GLOBAL DATABASE". MIDDLE FLOOR, split into TWO sealed rooms by a thick vertical sage-green wall running floor to ceiling, with a small padlock icon on the wall. The two admins cannot see each other; the wall is opaque. A faint dotted red line attempts to cross the wall and is blocked by the padlock (labelled tiny "a naming prefix is only a hope").

BOTTOM FLOOR (vault), tinted warm off-white (#f5eed9): a row of SEVEN small labelled safes/drawers, each drawn as a mini-firewall unit, labelled "device DB 1" through "device DB 7". Between the middle floor and this vault, two arrows: one pointing UP labelled "RETRIEVE (onboard first)" in green, one pointing DOWN labelled "INSTALL (day-2 push)" in blue.

Muted gold accents on the building's frame and floor numbers. Clean thin labels in a sans-serif. Generous spacing, calm and legible like an educational diagram. Colour palette: cream, coral (#e07a5f-ish), muted gold, sage green, soft brown outlines, ink-navy text.

DO NOT include: photorealism, gradients, glossy 3D rendering, dense technical jargon walls of text, real brand logos, or more than the labels specified. Keep it clean enough to read as a teaching diagram at a glance.

Title: 4 · The Prefix Is a Hope (ADOM vs naming)

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, warm cream background (#faf5e9), soft-brown outlines, no gradients, no 3D. A side-by-side comparison of two ways to "separate" two firms.

RIGHT PANEL, titled "A WALL": the same firewalls, but now split by a thick solid sage-green floor-to-ceiling wall with a padlock. Caption ribbon: "an ADOM is an administrative boundary — enforced".

Colour palette: cream, coral, muted gold, sage green, soft brown outlines, ink-navy text.

DO NOT include: photorealism, gradients, glossy 3D, real brand logos, offensive stereotype. The contrast between "sticky notes" and "solid locked wall" must be immediately obvious.

Title: 5 · The Global Test — Everyone, or Just Them?

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, warm cream background (#faf5e9), soft-brown outlines, no gradients, no 3D. A sorting/decision scene shaped like a fork in a conveyor belt.

A single object enters from the left on a conveyor, reaching a signpost that asks: "TRUE FOR EVERYONE?" Two chutes branch from it.

TOP CHUTE labelled "YES -> GLOBAL DATABASE": three objects (syslog, NTP, DNS icons) ride up into a soft-blue penthouse tray at the top, each getting a small coral "g-" tag, then fanning out via two "ASSIGN" arrows down into two ADOM rooms below. Caption: "define once, assign to all".

BOTTOM CHUTE labelled "JUST THIS TENANT -> LOCAL ADOM": a tenant-specific policy object drops neatly into one sealed sage-green ADOM room labelled "Vaultline", staying behind the wall.

To the RIGHT, a small cautionary inset panel with a faded red X, titled "ALL-GLOBAL = NO WALL": it shows the dividing wall between two ADOMs crumbling/dissolving into dashed lines because tenant-specific objects were pushed to global, with tiny caption "you may as well have one ADOM".

Colour palette: cream, coral, muted gold, sage green, soft brown outlines, ink-navy text. Clean, calm, legible like an educational flow diagram.

DO NOT include: photorealism, gradients, glossy 3D, real logos, dense text. The "everyone vs just them" fork must be the visual focus; the crumbling-wall inset should be small and clearly a warning.
