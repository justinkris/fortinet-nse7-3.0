THE RETRIEVAL-UNDER-PRESSURE PATTERN — DEDICATED TRACKING (Sessions 4, 7, 8, 9)
=========================================================================
Observed shape: a question requiring an instant verdict (T/F, "what's the first cause") on
material the student HAS already correctly reasoned through elsewhere sometimes produces a
first-pass answer that reaches for a plausible-sounding gut mechanism instead of retrieving
the specific, already-proven mechanism.
Timeline:
  Session 4/5 — dstintf retrieval needed prompting.
  Session 7 — Q2, correctly identified "traffic gets dropped" but needed prompting to name
              the exact failure stage (policy lookup, not routing).
  Session 8 — Q2, T/F on switch-interface bridging: first answer WRONG (guessed False,
              "there's a policy evaluation but it's a permit") despite having personally
              gathered contradicting evidence less than 30 minutes earlier in the same
              session. Self-corrected fast once pointed back at his own evidence.
  Session 9 — Q6, the SAME question shape (T/F on inter-VDOM link auto-functioning):
              INSTANT, CORRECT VERDICT, no hesitation, first time in four attempts.
RESULT AS OF SESSION 9: the gut-level VERDICT retrieval gap appears RESOLVED — this is a
genuine, measurable improvement, not just optimism; it took four data points across five
sessions to be confident of this and this is the first clean pass.
HOWEVER: a related but DISTINCT gap remains — when asked to justify a correct verdict in
detail (not just call True/False), the JUSTIFICATION can still reach for a plausible-but-
wrong specific mechanism (Session 9 Q6: "matching IP addressing" instead of "a route").
RECOMMENDATION FOR SEASON 2: track these as two separate sub-patterns from now on. Do not
re-test the verdict-level gap as if unresolved — that would understate real progress. DO
continue light-touch testing on the justification-detail layer, since that is the
newly-isolated remaining piece. Use the same technique that has worked every time it's been
applied: redirect toward the general principle/mechanism rather than supplying the answer.
