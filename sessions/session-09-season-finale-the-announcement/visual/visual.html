Session 9 · Visual Learning

Season Finale: The Announcement

Recommended visual style for this session: a blend of Real-World Analogy (for the VDOM chassis-binding idea, which is genuinely best explained through absurdity/comedy) and Packet Journey (for the literal WAN round-trip this session's central mistake would cause). Two placeholders below cover these; a third closes the season visually as a topology snapshot.

1. The Satellite Receptionist

Style: Real-World Analogy · Purpose: reinforce the chassis-binding VDOM model
[ IMAGE PLACEHOLDER ]

Why this image matters: The single most important idea of Season 1's finale — "a VDOM doesn't move a building" — is abstract until it's made physically absurd. This illustration should make the WASTE of the mistake viscerally obvious at a glance, anchoring the memory far better than a technical diagram would.

Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic vector-art illustration, storybook-educational style, cream/off-white background (#faf5e9), coral (#c1502e) and muted gold accents, sage green secondary tones, soft brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism, clean bold line work, generous whitespace. Composition: split-scene illustration divided by a long, winding dotted line representing a WAN link crossing a stylised map, connecting two labelled buildings — "PARRAMATTA" (small, modern office building, warm coral roof) on the left and "SYDNEY HQ" (larger building with a server-room icon and a small ice-cube-with-frozen-money-stacks logo, the LoFinance branding) on the right. Inside the Parramatta building: two small desk icons three units apart, each with a simple person silhouette, a speech-bubble between them with a small "?" icon, implying they're trying to talk to each other. A single thin coral arrow line leaves the LEFT desk, travels the full dotted WAN path all the way to a tiny labelled desk/server icon inside the HQ building marked "VDOM (hosted here)", loops around it, and travels the entire path BACK to the RIGHT desk in Parramatta — visually absurd and clearly much longer than the three-desk distance it should have taken. Small clock icon with motion lines near the looping arrow to suggest wasted time/latency. A small "3 desks apart" label with a tiny ruler icon directly between the two Parramatta desks, contrasted visually against the enormous loop. Mood: gently comedic, "well that's obviously silly" energy — not stern or overly technical. Muted gold highlight glow around the HQ VDOM icon to draw the eye to where the bottleneck actually is. No dense technical labels beyond what's specified above. No characters' faces needed (simple silhouettes only). No photorealism, no 3D, no glossy rendering. Learning purpose: make the WAN-round-trip absurdity of hosting a remote site's VDOM at HQ instantly, viscerally memorable — this is the season's single most important idea.
Aspect ratio: 3:2 Placement: Immediately after the Q4 drill explanation

Accessibility description: A split illustration showing two buildings, Parramatta and Sydney HQ, connected by a long WAN link. Two desks sit close together inside the Parramatta office, but a message between them is shown looping all the way to a VDOM hosted at HQ and back, illustrating wasted distance for local traffic.

2. Two Doors, Two Keys, Two Permission Slips

Style: Packet Journey · Purpose: reinforce inter-VDOM link route+policy requirement
[ IMAGE PLACEHOLDER ]

Why this image matters: Session 7-9 material consistently trips people on "the link exists, so traffic should just flow" — this visual makes the two-independent- requirements (route + policy, per side) concrete and countable.

Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic vector-art illustration, educational diagram style, cream background (#faf5e9), sage green and muted gold accents, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism. Composition: two simple labelled rooms side by side, "VDOM A" (left, pale blue wash) and "VDOM B" (right, pale coral wash), connected by a single doorway in the shared wall between them labelled "VDOM LINK" with small "0" and "1" tags on each side of the doorway frame. In front of each side of the doorway, draw a small locked door icon. Each locked door has TWO separate requirements shown as small attached tags: a tag shaped like a signpost/map-arrow labelled "ROUTE" and a tag shaped like a small permission slip/checkmark labelled "POLICY". Both tags must be present and highlighted (muted gold glow) on BOTH sides of the doorway for a packet icon (small envelope with a subtle motion trail) to be shown successfully passing through the middle. Add a small "✓ auto-created" label near the doorway frame itself (the 0/1 interfaces), separate and visually distinct from the two required tags, to show that the interface pair itself is not the missing piece. Mood: clean, diagrammatic, slightly playful (the locked-door metaphor), reassuring rather than intimidating — this should look like a solvable puzzle, not an error message. No dense technical CLI text. No FortiGate hardware iconography needed — this is a conceptual/logical diagram, not a physical rack diagram. Learning purpose: make "route AND policy, independently, on both sides" visually countable and memorable — this is a recurring exam trap.
Aspect ratio: 16:9 Placement: Alongside the Q6 drill explanation / inter-VDOM link config table

Accessibility description: Diagram of two rooms, VDOM A and VDOM B, connected by a doorway representing an inter-VDOM link. Each side of the doorway shows two required tags, a route and a policy, both of which must be present for a packet to pass through.

3. Season 1 Topology Snapshot

Style: Enterprise Topology Map · Purpose: consolidate the full Season 1 network state before Season 2 begins
[ IMAGE PLACEHOLDER ]

Why this image matters: Nine sessions of incremental changes deserve one consolidated mental snapshot before Parramatta adds a second site. This is memory consolidation, not new teaching.

Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic vector-art enterprise network map, storybook-educational style, cream background (#faf5e9), coral/gold/sage accent palette, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, generous spacing, no gradients, no photorealism. Composition: a single building outline labelled "SYDNEY HQ" containing one central FortiGate icon (simple rounded rectangle with a small shield emblem). From the FortiGate, draw clearly separated branches to: a "TRADING FLOOR" zone (VLAN 20) and an "EXEC FLOOR" zone (VLAN 5), each behind its own small lock icon on the connecting line; a "FINANCE" zone (VLAN 10) and a "SUPPORT" zone (VLAN 30), each also behind a small lock icon; and two dashed-outline sub-boxes drawn INSIDE the same FortiGate icon (to show they're logical partitions of the same box, not separate hardware) labelled "TRADING VDOM" and "GUEST-WIFI VDOM", connected to each other and to the root by a small labelled bridge icon marked "VDOM LINK (route + policy)". Small icons: a tiny sandwich icon tucked in a corner near a "SEÑOR" label (subtle, not a character illustration, just an icon signaling his presence conceptually), a tiny sombrero icon near it. At the very edge of the illustration, outside the HQ building outline, add a faint, small, unlabeled dotted-line stub heading off-canvas toward the edge of the page with a small grey question-mark icon — representing Parramatta as not-yet-built, deliberately vague and unlabeled (no Parramatta details, this is a Season 1 close, not a Season 2 preview). Mood: calm, organized, "everything in its place" — a sense of completion and readiness, not tension. No dense technical labels beyond the zone names and VDOM names specified. No real IP addresses. No characters' faces. Learning purpose: consolidate the full, correct Season 1 network topology into one memorable image before Season 2 introduces a second physical site.
Aspect ratio: 16:9 Placement: End of page, closing visual

Accessibility description: A network topology map of Sydney HQ showing a central FortiGate connected to Trading, Exec, Finance, and Support VLAN zones, each behind a lock icon, plus Trading and Guest-WiFi VDOMs shown as partitions inside the same FortiGate, linked by a labelled VDOM link. A faint unlabeled dotted line hints at a future, not-yet-built connection.
