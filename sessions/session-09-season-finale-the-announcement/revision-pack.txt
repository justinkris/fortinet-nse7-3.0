CHEAT SHEET
------------
Firewall roles (NGFW/ISFW/DCFW/DEFW): role = POSITION + TRAFFIC PATH, not hardware/OS.
  One FortiOS, any role, depending on placement.
VLAN segmentation is real only when: default gateway lives on the FIREWALL (not a switch).
  Why: policy match needs a known dstintf; routing must resolve that before policy can
  evaluate anything.
802.1Q trunk: EGRESS adds the tag (leaving the FortiGate onto the trunk), INGRESS removes it
  (arriving at the FortiGate).
VDOM types: NAT mode = routed, own IPs, real hop. Transparent mode = L2 bridge, invisible,
  no routing.
Inter-VDOM link: creating the link ≠ done. Need, on BOTH sides, independently:
  1. a ROUTE (own per-VDOM routing table)
  2. a POLICY (own per-VDOM policy table)
  VDOM-link 0/1 interfaces auto-address themselves at creation — that part is NOT something
  you configure.
THE BIG IDEA: "A VDOM doesn't move a building." Chassis-bound, not location-independent.
Design rule: before evaluating a logical shortcut across sites, ask "where does the physical
  hardware actually sit, and what traffic would have to travel to reach it?"

FLASHCARDS
-----------
Q: What determines a FortiGate's role (NGFW/ISFW/DCFW/DEFW)?
A: Its position in the network and the traffic that crosses it there — not hardware/licence.

Q: What makes a VLAN "real" segmentation rather than just a label?
A: The default gateway lives on the firewall, forcing routing + policy evaluation for
   inter-VLAN traffic.

Q: Why does routing have to resolve before a policy can match traffic?
A: Policy matching requires a known destination interface (dstintf); routing is what
   determines that interface.

Q: Relative to the FortiGate, which direction adds an 802.1Q tag, and which removes it?
A: Egress adds it (leaving onto the trunk); ingress removes it (arriving at the FortiGate).

Q: What's the core flaw in hosting a remote site's VDOM on the HQ chassis?
A: The VDOM doesn't relocate the chassis — local traffic at the remote site would have to
   cross the WAN twice just to reach the VDOM and come back.

Q: What two things does an inter-VDOM link need, independently, on BOTH sides, beyond just
   creating the link?
A: A route (per-VDOM routing table) and a policy (per-VDOM policy table).

Q: NAT mode VDOM vs Transparent mode VDOM — core difference?
A: NAT mode routes (own IP scheme, acts as a hop); Transparent mode bridges at layer 2
   (invisible, no routing).

Q: Why can't a remote site's VLAN simply join an HQ VDOM's config?
A: A VLAN tag only has meaning on the physical trunk/switch stack it's declared on; a VDOM
   only sees traffic on its own chassis's interfaces. No physical Layer 2 path connects them.

Keep technical explanations accurate to real FortiGate/FortiOS behaviour. Do not invent
commands or config syntax not covered in the source material. End with a short, clear
