5-Minute Revision

Season Finale: The Announcement

Roles

NGFW / ISFW / DCFW / DEFW

Not hardware. Role = position + traffic crossing it there. One FortiOS wears any hat depending on placement. Small branch edge, securing the WAN link = DEFW.

VLANs

What makes segmentation real

Gateway must live on the firewall, not a switch — forces routing + policy evaluation. Policy match needs a known dstintf, so routing resolves first.

VLANs

802.1Q tagging

Trunk port carries multiple VLANs on one cable. Egress adds the tag, ingress removes it — relative to the FortiGate.

"A VDOM doesn't move a building." A VDOM is bound to the physical chassis it lives in. Carve a Parramatta VDOM out of the HQ box, and even local Parramatta traffic has to cross the WAN twice to reach it.
VDOMs

NAT mode vs Transparent mode

NAT mode = routed, own IPs, acts as a real hop. Transparent mode = layer-2 bridge, invisible, no routing.

VDOMs

Inter-VDOM link — the trap

Creating the link is NOT enough. Both sides need, independently: a route (own routing table) + a policy (own policy table). VDOM-link 0/1 interfaces auto-address themselves — that part's free.

Synthesis

VLANs and VDOMs share a boundary

A VLAN tag only means something on the trunk it's declared on. A VDOM only sees traffic on its own chassis. Both are physically bound — you can't route around geography with config alone.
