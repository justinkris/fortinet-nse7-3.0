SESSION 15 · VISUAL LEARNING PAGE

The Packet Journey: One Hello, Three Tunnels

Recommended style for this session: Packet Journey — because the entire diagnosis hinged on following one multicast hello to its death, and the fix is understood by following its unicast replacements to their destinations. A secondary Real-World Analogy panel reinforces the DR insight.

Why "Packet Journey" for this session

Session 15's core mental model — a network type is a set of assumptions about the medium — is inherently spatial. The broadcast failure and the P2MP fix differ only in what happens to a packet at one interface. Watching that moment, twice, is worth more than any table. The visuals below trace: the hello's death, its unicast resurrection, the impossible election, the neighbor-table fingerprint, and the redistribution valve.

IMAGE PLACEHOLDER 1 — The Shout Into a Deaf Interface
1 · The Shout Into a Deaf Interface
Purpose: anchor WHY broadcast mode fails silently · Aspect ratio 16:9 · Placement: top of page, first concept
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, modern storybook-educational style, cream/warm off-white background, coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism. Wide 16:9 composition, slightly elevated three-quarter view. Left third: a stylised FortiGate hub firewall drawn as a sturdy blue-grey appliance labelled "HUB — To-Spokes", standing on a wooden staging bench inside the LoFinance server room; the LoFinance logo (stacks of banknotes frozen inside a translucent ice cube) appears on a small wall plaque. From the hub's single tunnel interface, a large speech-bubble-shaped packet labelled "HELLO → 224.0.0.5" is being shouted through a vintage brass megaphone attached to the port. The sound waves radiate outward — and immediately dissolve into faint dotted arcs and small falling ash particles the instant they leave the port, because there is NO wire, NO switch, NO shared medium in the middle of the frame: just empty cream space with a hand-lettered word "DEAF." floating where a LAN segment would be. Right third: three separate armored pipe-like encrypted tunnels (each with a small padlock icon) curve away toward three distant tiny buildings labelled Parramatta, DR, Chatswood — visibly disconnected from the dissolving sound waves. In the far corner, small Junior character (youthful, spiky hair, LoFinance polo, sheepish expression) holds a printed blog post. Mood: quiet, ironic failure. Must NOT include: any DR crown icons, any successful arrows reaching the spokes, dense CLI text. Learning purpose: broadcast's multicast hello relies on a shared medium for delivery; with none present and no per-tunnel replication, the hello dies at the interface — hence an EMPTY neighbor table.
Accessibility: A firewall shouts a multicast hello through a megaphone into empty space labelled "Deaf"; the sound dissolves before reaching three separate locked tunnels leading to three spoke sites.
Memory anchor: empty table = the hello was never delivered. The image freezes the exact moment of non-delivery.
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER 2 — The Courier With Three Copies
2 · Point-to-Multipoint: The Courier With Three Copies
Purpose: show the P2MP mechanism (replicate + unicast per spoke) · Aspect ratio 16:9 · Placement: immediately after Placeholder 1 as the "fix" panel
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, same consistent style: cream background, coral accents, muted gold, sage green, soft brown outlines, bold clean lines, no gradients, no 3D. Wide 16:9, mirror composition of the previous image so the before/after pairing is obvious. The same blue-grey FortiGate hub labelled "HUB — To-Spokes (point-to-multipoint)" now has, instead of a megaphone, a small cheerful courier desk at its interface: a stylised postal counter where a single envelope labelled "HELLO" is being run through a duplicating stamp machine that outputs THREE identical envelopes, each addressed with a small tag — "→ Parramatta", "→ DR", "→ Chatswood". Three delivery arms place one envelope into each of the three armored padlocked tunnels, and each envelope is shown mid-journey inside its own pipe, clearly separated, each arriving at its own small spoke building where a tiny spoke firewall accepts it with a green check mark. Above each tunnel, a thin sage-green thread labelled "adjacency: FULL" connects hub to that spoke only — no threads run spoke-to-spoke; the space between spoke buildings is deliberately empty with a faint crossed-out dotted line and the caption "spokes never hear each other's hellos". Small Señor character (sombrero, immaculate posture, chaotic desk just visible at frame edge) watches approvingly with arms folded. Must NOT include: any multicast waves, any DR/BDR crowns, any spoke-to-spoke arrows. Learning purpose: P2MP treats one interface as a bundle of individual point-to-point conversations — it replicates and unicasts per spoke, forming exactly one adjacency per spoke.
Accessibility: The same firewall now duplicates one hello envelope into three addressed copies, delivering one down each tunnel to its own spoke; green "FULL" threads connect hub to each spoke, with no connections between spokes.
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER 3 — The Election Nobody Can Attend
3 · The Election Nobody Can Attend
Purpose: make "P2MP holds no DR election" un-forgettable via analogy · Aspect ratio 4:3 · Placement: beside the DR/DROther discussion
Image Generation Prompt
Flat detailed vector illustration, consistent series style (cream background, coral, muted gold, sage, soft brown outlines, no gradients). 4:3 composition, split into two halves by a hand-drawn dividing line. LEFT HALF, captioned "BROADCAST LAN — election works": a cosy town-hall meeting room where five anthropomorphised router characters sit around one shared oval table (the table itself subtly drawn as an Ethernet switch with port markings); one router wears a small gold crown labelled "DR", another a silver crown labelled "BDR", a third holds a modest ribbon labelled "DROther — ran & lost"; ballot slips are visible on the shared table — everyone can see and hear everyone. RIGHT HALF, captioned "HUB & SPOKE — no election possible": three router characters each locked inside their own separate soundproof glass phone booth (the booths are the tunnels, each with a padlock), all three booths wired ONLY to a central operator desk where the hub router sits with a switchboard; the routers in booths hold ballot slips but face blank walls — thought bubbles show question marks; a large official notice pinned to the operator desk reads "ELECTION CANCELLED — VOTERS CANNOT HEAR EACH OTHER". The role board behind the operator shows three neighbor entries each marked "Full / —" with an elegant dash. Mood: gently comic, clear. Must NOT include: a crowned hub (the hub must NOT wear a DR crown — that was the student's trap), any spoke-to-spoke wiring. Learning purpose: DROther is a campaign result that requires an election; on a star topology no election can run, so the role column honestly shows a dash.
Accessibility: Split scene — a LAN town hall where routers elect a DR around one shared table, versus a hub-and-spoke switchboard where spokes sit in isolated booths and a notice declares the election cancelled; the roster shows "Full / —".
Memory anchor: the hub does not "win" the election — the election is never held. If you ever imagine crowning the hub, picture the cancelled-election notice.

Accessibility: A detective's magnifying glass examines the dash in OSPF neighbor output; fingerprint evidence cards map each role value to the network type it implies.
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER 5 — The Guarded Valve
5 · Redistribution: The Guarded Valve
Purpose: encode default-closed redistribution + access-list filtering + blast radius · Aspect ratio 16:9 · Placement: with the redistribution/filtering section
Image Generation Prompt
Flat detailed vector illustration, consistent series style (cream, coral, muted gold, sage, soft brown outlines, no gradients). Wide 16:9 industrial-waterworks metaphor. LEFT: a private reservoir tank labelled "STATIC ROUTES (hub-local)" containing several floating labelled droplets: "10.99.50.0/24 MGMT" (sage green, glowing softly), plus murkier coral droplets labelled "emergency static", "unencrypted default 0.0.0.0/0", "forgotten test route". CENTRE: a large brass pipe valve labelled "redistribute static — DEFAULT: CLOSED" with a prominent wheel; the valve is now partly open, but bolted immediately after it is a fine mesh filter grate in a frame stamped "ACCESS-LIST: MGMT-ONLY · exact-match" — the single green droplet passes through the mesh into the outflow, while the coral droplets are visibly stopped flat against the grate. RIGHT: the outflow pipe fans into a wide irrigation network feeding three small spoke-site fields labelled Parramatta, DR, Chatswood — every channel automatically carrying the one green droplet, with a caption "OSPF floods it EVERYWHERE, automatically, trusted". Small inset vignette in the top corner, sepia-toned flashback style: a burst pipe flooding all three fields with coral water, captioned "Parramatta precedent — why the valve ships closed". A tiny ASBR badge is pinned to the valve station itself. Must NOT include: route-map machinery or extra filter stages (the point is ONE simple grate suffices). Learning purpose: redistribution is a valve that is closed by default because OSPF's automatic domain-wide trust makes an open valve a blast radius; a one-line access list is the sufficient guard for a one-prefix requirement.
Accessibility: A closed-by-default valve releases static routes from a tank; a mesh filter lets only the green management subnet through into irrigation channels that automatically feed all three spoke sites, while a flashback vignette shows the flood that unfiltered redistribution would cause.

How to use this page

Before the exam, walk the journey in order: the shout that dies (broadcast), the courier with three copies (P2MP), the cancelled election (no DR), the fingerprint ledger (Full/ -), the guarded valve (redistribution + filter), and the three hats (compound roles). Each image is one exam answer wearing a costume.
