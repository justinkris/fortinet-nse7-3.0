Title: The Night Shift Relay

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, modern storybook aesthetic, cream/warm off-white background, coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism. A nighttime relay race across a wide moonlit track inside a stylised office campus, deep indigo night sky with a large pale moon reading "03:04 AM" on its face like a clock. Five runners in sequence, left to right, each passing a glowing amber baton shaped like a log-file scroll. Runner 1: a small laptop character with legs wearing a "FortiClient" running bib, sweating slightly, handing the scroll-baton to Runner 2: a tall librarian figure made of stacked log books wearing glasses and a "FortiAnalyzer" bib, holding a magnifying glass over the scroll revealing a red skull watermark labelled "IoC". Runner 3: a sturdy castle-gate/firewall character with a "FortiGate" bib, pausing mid-stride to check a clipboard titled "CONNECTED?" with a green tick. Runner 4: a manager figure with a headset and megaphone, "EMS" bib, shouting a speech bubble containing a padlock icon toward Runner 5. Runner 5 is the SAME laptop character from the start, now standing still inside a glowing sage-green quarantine bubble, calmly handcuffing ITSELF with tiny padlock cuffs, holding up a small flag reading "STATUS: QUARANTINED" that points back toward runners 3 and 4 with two thin dotted return-arrows. Numbered coral medallions 1–5 above each handoff. In the far background, darkened office windows with one lit window showing a sleeping engineer, sombrero on a hook beside the desk. LoFinance logo (stacks of money frozen inside an ice cube) on a small stadium banner. Composition: single horizontal track, strong left-to-right reading order, generous spacing. Must NOT appear: dense technical labels, vendor logos other than the bib names, gore, extra runners.

Title: The Author, Not the Dispatcher

Prompt:
Flat detailed vector illustration, cream background, coral/gold/sage palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, storybook-professional tone. Split scene. LEFT half, labelled with a small gold ribbon "CONFIG TIME": a grand old-fashioned printing press shaped like a crowned firewall tower (small crown on top, label "ROOT — HQ-NGFW"), operated by Señor — a warm, eccentric senior engineer with a sombrero, rolled sleeves, chaotic desk of cables beside him, immaculate network diagram on the wall behind. The press is stamping identical small booklets titled "IF / THEN" and three conveyor arrows carry one booklet each to three smaller firewall-tower characters on the right labelled "HQ-ISFW", "HQ-DCFW", "BRANCH". Each small tower tucks its booklet into its own chest pocket. RIGHT half, labelled "TRIGGER TIME — 03:00": the same three small towers now under a night sky, each calmly reading from ITS OWN pocket booklet by lamplight and taking action (one photographing a CPU flame with a camera, one stamping a backup ledger, one holding the green quarantine bubble from Visual 1 in miniature) — while the crowned printing press in the corner is dark, unplugged, with a "ZZZ" sleep bubble and a small bandage, clearly offline. A sage-green banner across the bottom: "PUSH AT CONFIG TIME, NOT TRIGGER TIME." Visual hierarchy: press dominant on left, three independent towers dominant on right, sleeping press small but unmistakable. Must NOT appear: wires connecting the sleeping press to the acting towers (independence is the whole point), extra firewalls, dense CLI text.
