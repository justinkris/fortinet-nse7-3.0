QUICK SUMMARY (one page)
------------------------------------------------------------------
A substation fault put LoFinance on 12 minutes of UPS behind a
single 900G. Penny funded HA. FGCP joins 2+ FortiGates into one
brain: configuration sync, session sync, and one virtual identity
(vMAC/IP) so failover is invisible.

MODES: active-passive (one works, one waits, fully synced) vs
active-active (primary owns the vMACs, receives every session
FIRST, and load-balances some to the partner — those sessions pay
an inter-chassis hop both directions for life; value is mainly for
inspection-heavy CP/CPU work). LoFinance chose a-p to protect the
NP Direct trading path.

REQUIREMENTS — blockers (no cluster if mismatched): same MODEL,
FIRMWARE, HARD-DRIVE CONFIG, OPERATING MODE (mgmt VDOM NAT/
transparent). Setup identity must also match: group-id, group-name,
password, hbdev settings. THE EXCEPTION: licensing — cluster forms
anyway but runs at the LOWEST license level of any member.

HEARTBEAT: ≥2 hb interfaces (higher number = higher priority;
direct-connect 2-member pairs; DHCP/PPPoE→static during setup).
One silent heartbeat risks SPLIT-BRAIN: both members promote, one
identity duplicated, network chaos. Ethertypes (not IP):
0x8890/0x8891 heartbeat-election-failure-detection; 0x8893 config
sync.

ELECTION, override DISABLED (default), stops at first decider:
  1. Connected monitored interfaces (most up wins)
  2. HA uptime (highest wins ONLY if gap >= 5 minutes)
  3. Priority (highest)
  4. Serial number (highest)
HA uptime resets to 0 on monitored-iface failure or reboot.
Graceful failover with zero config change: diagnose sys ha
reset-uptime ON THE PRIMARY.

Override ENABLED (CLI ONLY): priority jumps above uptime. Benefit:
deterministic preferred primary. Bill: recovered ex-primary takes
the role back = a guaranteed SECOND failover per incident; a
flapping member becomes a failover generator. Override + priority
are NOT synchronised — set per member. Graceful failover lever
here = change priorities.

CHEAT SHEET
------------------------------------------------------------------
CLI
  config system ha
      set mode a-p | a-a
      set group-id <0-255>        (must match)
      set group-name "<name>"     (must match)
      set password <secret>       (must match)
      set hbdev "<if1>" 50 "<if2>" 50   (higher # = higher prio)
      set override enable         (CLI only; per member; not synced)
      set priority <n>            (per member; not synced)
  end
  diagnose sys ha reset-uptime            (on primary; graceful failover)
  diagnose sys ha dump-by vcluster        (uptime/reset_cnt standings;
                                           uptime 0 = LOWEST member)
  diagnose sniffer packet any "ether proto 0x8890" 4
  get system ha status / get system status

DEFAULTS & RULES
  Override: disabled by default.
  Uptime rule: must lead by >= 5 minutes to decide the election.
  Election stops at the first criterion that picks a winner.
  Licensing mismatch: forms, degrades to lowest level.
  Model/firmware/HDD/op-mode mismatch: does NOT form.
  Ethertypes: 8890/8891 alive · 8893 identical.
  A-A packet path: primary first, always; partner sessions hop.

DESIGN RULES
  >=2 heartbeat links. Direct-connect 2-member hb.
  Match licenses before the purchase order.
  Mode choice follows the packet path, not the price tag.
  Override only when a business reason demands a fixed primary and
  can afford failback failovers.

FLASHCARDS (Q -> A)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Four member requirements that block FGCP formation?
A: Same model, firmware, hard-drive config, operating mode.

Q: What happens when cluster members have different licenses?
A: Cluster still forms; runs at the lowest license level.

Q: Election order, override disabled?
A: Monitored interfaces > HA uptime (>=5 min gap) > priority > serial.

Q: Election order, override enabled?
A: Monitored interfaces > priority > HA uptime > serial.

Q: Where do you configure override?
A: CLI only; per member; not synchronised (nor is priority).

Q: Graceful failover, default settings, no config change?
A: diagnose sys ha reset-uptime — on the primary.

Q: dump-by vcluster shows uptime/reset_cnt=0/1 — meaning?
A: This member has the LOWEST HA uptime (0 is relative) and its
   uptime has been reset once.

Q: What resets HA uptime to 0?
A: Monitored interface failure, reboot, or manual reset-uptime.

Q: FGCP ethertypes and their jobs?
A: 0x8890/0x8891 heartbeat/election/failure-detection;
   0x8893 configuration sync. ("88-90 alive, 88-93 identical.")

Q: Why at least two heartbeat interfaces?
A: A silent heartbeat causes split-brain: both members promote and
   duplicate the shared vMAC/IP identity.

Q: In a-a, where does every new session arrive first?
A: The primary (it owns the virtual MACs); partner-assigned
   sessions pay an inter-chassis hop both ways for life.

Q: Cost of enabling override?
A: A guaranteed second failover (failback) whenever the preferred
   primary recovers; flapping member = perpetual failovers.

Q: True/false: override guarantees fewer failovers?
A: FALSE — it guarantees more (failback per incident).

Q: DHCP/PPPoE interfaces during cluster setup?
A: Switch to static during initial formation; revert after.

Q: Manual quarantine of an endpoint — where do you type, and which
   OS is excluded? (register debt)
A: ON the FortiGate: diagnose endpoint fctems queue-complete-calls
   Q-<endpoint_ip>; EMS delivers it. FortiClient (Linux) excluded.

PODCAST PROMPT — "THE PACKET PATH" · EPISODE 45
------------------------------------------------------------------
Generate a 10–14 minute podcast episode of "The Packet Path", a
narrative networking show. Two hosts: a storyteller and an
engineer. Episode title: "The Night the Lights Went Out."

STORY: At 2:47 AM a substation fault drops a Sydney trading firm,
LoFinance, onto UPS power. The runtime gauge falls to 12 minutes
before the street comes back. Every trade runs through ONE
FortiGate. The next morning the ops director, Penny, asks the
question that finally funds high availability: "What if it
hadn't?" The junior engineer wants to buy a second box by Friday —
including, at one point, a discounted DIFFERENT model. The mentor,
Señor (a man in a sombrero who communicates approval by putting
his coffee DOWN), forces the team to understand clustering before
purchasing.

TEACH, through the story, in this order:
1. FGCP as a shared brain: config sync + session sync + one
   virtual MAC/IP identity, so failover is invisible.
2. Requirements: same model, firmware, hard-drive config,
   operating mode — or no cluster. Licensing is the twist: the
   cluster forms but runs at the LOWEST license level. Analogy:
   "the cluster runs at the level of its poorest member" — an
   unlicensed second box effectively refunds the licenses on the
   first.
3. Heartbeats and split-brain: why two heartbeat links; ethertypes
   0x8890/0x8891 (alive) and 0x8893 (identical).
4. Primary election with override off: monitored interfaces first
   ("facts about now beat preferences from Tuesday"), then HA
   uptime with the five-minute rule, then priority, then serial
   ("twins settle it by birth certificate"). The graceful-failover
   trick: diagnose sys ha reset-uptime on the primary — no config
   change.
5. Override: CLI-only, priority jumps above uptime, deterministic
   primary — but "every guarantee has a bill": a second failover
   when the old primary recovers, and a flapping box becomes a
   failover machine. Override and priority don't sync between
   members.
6. The mode decision: in active-active, every session hits the
   primary FIRST, and partner-balanced sessions pay an
   inter-chassis hop both directions for life — "the toll booth
   in a redundancy costume" for a firm that just spent a season
   removing an internal hop to win back 2.1 milliseconds. They
   choose active-passive: "the idle twin isn't idle — it's
   insurance with a sub-second claims process."

JUNIOR'S MISTAKES to dramatise: the bargain 600F (same model or
nothing), "active-active because we paid for two", and enabling
override "in the GUI, on one member" (no GUI option exists, and
override/priority never synchronise).

END with the teaser: the second unit is on a pallet, and tomorrow
the mentor will explain "the MAC address that does not exist on
any interface — yet owns all the traffic."

Tone: warm, cinematic, technically precise. Use the analogies
verbatim. Close with the mentor placing his sombrero on the crate.
