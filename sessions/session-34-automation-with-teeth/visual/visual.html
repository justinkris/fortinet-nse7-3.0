Visual Learning · Session 34

The Packet Path of a Config Change

Follow one edit from template to fleet — and see why one character reaches all thirty.

Every change is a journey through layers and one single door.
If you can picture the journey, you can predict what the screen will say before you click.
Recommended Visual Style

Why a Change Journey

Chosen style: Packet Journey / Process Flow (through checkpoints)
This session is fundamentally about a change travelling — from a template or script, down through the layers, through the single door of the device database, out to the fleet. A journey-through-checkpoints visual makes the "assign stages / install ships" law and the "single door" insight physically obvious. Paired with a blast-radius illustration for "one character, thirty firewalls."

The Blast Radius of a Shared Object

Image Placeholder — Blast Radius
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalist vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), soft-brown outlines, no gradients, storybook-technical. Centre: a single large sheet of paper labelled "CLI TEMPLATE", with a magnified line of code reading: set subnet $(dmz_naem) — the misspelled word "dmz_naem" circled in coral with a small coral "TYPO" tag and a broken-pointer icon (a bent arrow that points at nothing). Beside it, a small tidy dictionary/book icon labelled "METADATA VARIABLES" with a correct green entry "dmz_name" — visually intact, to show the dictionary is RIGHT while the pointer is BENT. From the single template sheet, thirty thin coral lines fan outward to thirty small identical firewall appliances arranged in an arc, EVERY one of them showing the same amber warning triangle — emphasising that one shared object propagates one fault to all thirty identically. A muted-gold caption ribbon reads: "one character. thirty firewalls." In the lower corner, a small inset shows a hand reaching for a seatbelt-shaped "$" picker button, with a caption "the guardrail he declined". Colour palette: cream, LoFinance deep blue devices, coral for the fault and fan-out lines, muted gold captions, sage green only on the correct dictionary entry, amber triangles on devices. Small frozen-money-in-ice-cube LoFinance logo in a corner. Clean, uncluttered, teaching-diagram legibility. DO NOT include: photorealism, gore/drama, dense config dumps, more than the one code line. Learning purpose: burn in that the template and the metadata entry are SINGLE SHARED OBJECTS, so any typo hits the whole fleet — and that the $ picker prevents exactly this.
Why this matters: "Automation removes variance, not mistakes" — the fan-out makes the asymmetry unforgettable and shows the dictionary-vs-reference distinction in one frame.
Accessibility: One template with a circled typo fanning a single fault out to thirty identical firewalls, beside a correct dictionary entry, illustrating that shared objects share their mistakes.

The Stencil That Always Wins

Pre-Run vs Continuous
Aspect ratio 3:2 · place after Concept 3 · distinguish the two flavours
Image Placeholder — Two Stencils
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalist vector illustration, cream background, soft-brown outlines, no gradients, storybook-technical. Split into two side-by-side panels sharing a central divider. LEFT PANEL titled "PRE-RUN": a stencil pressing a pattern onto a single sheet ONCE, then lifting away and dissolving into faint dashed outline (labelled "auto-unassigns — gone"). A small ZTP/onboarding tag ("new device, day one"). Muted-gold caption: "fire once, then detaches." RIGHT PANEL titled "CONTINUOUS": the same stencil, but drawn firmly bolted above a sheet with a repeating stamp motion (three faint echo images showing it pressing again and again). Below the sheet, a small hand tries to erase/edit the pattern by hand, and an arrow shows the stencil pressing down and OVERWRITING the hand-edit. Coral caption: "stays bound — overwrites every install." A small toothy/gear motif near the title reinforces the word "teeth." Central divider carries a vertical label: "same power, opposite lifespan." Palette: cream, deep-blue stencils, coral for the continuous/overwrite action, sage green for the clean pre-run result, muted gold captions. Small LoFinance ice-cube logo in a corner. Legible teaching diagram, generous whitespace. DO NOT: photorealism, clutter, gradients. Learning purpose: make the auto-unassign vs stay-bound distinction physical, and show WHY you cannot out-edit a continuous template from below.
Why this matters: the exam trap lives here — "enforce / correct drift" means continuous. The repeating stamp makes "keeps winning" concrete.
Accessibility: Two panels: a pre-run stencil that stamps once and vanishes, versus a continuous stencil bolted in place that re-stamps and overwrites any hand-edit below it.
