SESSION 41 · VISUAL LEARNING PAGE

The Fast Path — a Packet Journey

Recommended style: Packet Journey — this session is literally the life story of a packet, told twice: once on the slow path, once on the fast path. A journey visual anchors the birth/life/death model and the toll gates (disqualifiers) better than any static diagram.

Scene 1 · The Birth

The CPU Witnesses the Handshake

Every session begins in the CPU's office. TCP brings three visitors (SYN, SYN/ACK, ACK); UDP brings one. Only after the CPU stamps the paperwork — policy, route, session — does it hand a brass key to the NP.

Image 1 — "The Border Office" (session birth)
Aspect ratio 16:9 · Place below Scene 1 text · Purpose: anchor the rule that the CPU must witness the TCP 3-way handshake / first UDP packet and then installs the session key.
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, cream/warm off-white background, coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism, storybook-professional educational style. Scene: a cross-section of a stylised FortiGate drawn as a small border-control building with two lanes. In the left lane, three cartoon envelope-characters labelled SYN, SYN/ACK and ACK queue politely at a wooden desk marked "CPU — SESSIONS OFFICE", where a bespectacled clerk-robot (the CPU) stamps a ledger titled "POLICY ✓ ROUTE ✓ SESSION ✓". In the right lane, a single envelope labelled "UDP #1" is being stamped in one motion. Behind the clerk, a wall of tiny brass keys; the clerk hands ONE glowing brass key tagged "SESSION KEY" through a hatch to a muscular courier-robot wearing a jersey printed "NP7", who waits beside a wide, empty express lane marked "FAST PATH" with chevron floor markings. A small sombrero hangs on a coat hook near the desk (subtle Señor cue) and a tiny frozen-money ice-cube logo (stacks of cash inside an ice cube, LoFinance branding) is etched on the office window. Composition: eye-level, slight left-to-right flow, generous spacing, clear visual hierarchy: queue → desk → key → express lane. Labels only where listed. Must NOT appear: dense technical text, vendor logos other than the fictional LoFinance cube, gradients, 3D gloss. Learning purpose: TCP needs the whole handshake witnessed, UDP needs one packet, and the reward is the session key installed in hardware.
Scene 2 · The Life

Riding the NP — What Gets Skipped

With the key installed, every subsequent packet bypasses the offices entirely. Routing? Pre-answered. Kernel? Asleep. CPU? Never sees it. The NP still performs the integrity pat-down at the door, and can still handle the trench coat (IPsec) and the speed limiter (shaping).

Scene 3 · The Toll Gates

Four Gates That Refuse the Key

Not every session earns a key. Four gates turn traffic back to the slow path — and one famous traveller in a trench coat is waved through anyway.

Image 3 — "The Four Gatekeepers" (offload disqualifiers)
Aspect ratio 3:2 · Place below Scene 3 text · Purpose: lock the four no-offload conditions and the IPsec exception as characters, not a list (list-facts fragment; story-facts stick — per the knowledge register).
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, same palette and continuity (cream, coral, muted gold, sage, soft brown outlines, no gradients). Scene: the entrance to the express lane from Images 1–2, guarded by four distinct gatekeeper characters standing at four coral turnstiles, each rejecting a specific envelope-packet back toward a dull grey staircase labelled "SLOW PATH — CPU". Gate 1: a clipboard-holding inspector pointing at a big toggle switch set to OFF, sign "OFFLOAD DISABLED ON POLICY"; a sheepish young intern character (Junior: youthful, overconfident posture deflated, help-desk badge) stands beside the switch holding a packet-sniffer net, having clearly forgotten it. Gate 2: a librarian-like character buffering a whole stack of envelopes into a reading tray, sign "PROXY PROFILES", with a small placard "ONE PROXY POISONS THE WHOLE POLICY" and a half-flow half-proxy striped envelope ALSO being rejected. Gate 3: a babysitter character spoon-feeding a fussy envelope wearing an "FTP" bib, sign "SESSION HELPER". Gate 4: a coat-check attendant refusing envelopes wrapped in wrappers labelled "SSL VPN" and "GRE", sign "TUNNELS" — BUT beside gate 4, a confident envelope in a smart beige trench coat labelled "IPsec" is being cheerfully waved THROUGH a golden side-door onto the fast path by the NP7 courier-robot, small banner reading "EXCEPT IPsec". A figure in a sombrero (Señor) observes from a balcony with quiet approval. Composition: four gates evenly spaced, rejected packets drifting toward the grey stairs, the single golden exception door visually distinct. Must NOT appear: real vendor logos, gradients, more than the listed labels. Learning purpose: the four disqualifiers plus the tunnel exception, encoded as memorable characters.
Scene 4 · The Death — and the Trap

FIN Returns to the Office; Stale Sessions Keep Their Verdict

When a FIN arrives, even the express lane sends it upstairs — only the CPU can close the ledger and take back the key. And the twist that fooled Junior: keys are judged once, at birth. Fix the rules today and yesterday's sessions still ride yesterday's verdict, until you clear them.

Image 4 — "The Ledger Closes" (teardown + stale-session remediation)
Aspect ratio 4:3 · Place below Scene 4 text · Purpose: bind FIN/RST-to-CPU and the session-clear remediation into one memory scene (this exact pair failed same-day retrieval — highest-value image of the set).
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, same palette and character continuity. Split scene, two halves divided by a soft brown vertical line. LEFT HALF, titled in small caps "THE DEATH": a single envelope-packet stamped "FIN" climbs the grey stairs from the express lane back up to the CPU clerk's desk from Image 1; the clerk-robot solemnly closes the big ledger, crosses out a session line, and retrieves the brass SESSION KEY from the NP7 courier, hanging it back on the key wall. Small caption plaque: "TEARDOWN IS STATE WORK". RIGHT HALF, titled "THE TRAP": a wall of pigeonholes labelled "SESSION TABLE" holds many older envelopes, each wearing a faded tag "NO OFFLOAD — VERDICT AT BIRTH"; above them a freshly repainted rulebook labelled "POLICY: OFFLOAD ENABLED ✓" — clearly newer than the tags. The engineer character (competent, calm, increasingly trusted — the Intermediate Engineer) sweeps the pigeonholes clean with a broom labelled "diagnose sys session clear", while new envelopes stream to the clerk's desk to be re-stamped and finally receive keys, small green checkmarks "offload=9/9" floating above the re-born sessions. Penny (operationally professional, folder under arm) watches a small latency gauge needle drop from red to sage green. LoFinance ice-cube logo on the ledger cover. Must NOT appear: gradients, photorealism, extra CLI text beyond the two labels listed. Learning purpose: FIN/RST always return to the CPU; config changes never re-judge existing sessions — clear them, reconnect, verify.
