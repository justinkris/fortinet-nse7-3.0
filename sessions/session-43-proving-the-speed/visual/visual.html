VISUAL LEARNING · SESSION 43

The Cold Case of El Túnel

A twenty-six-session-old defect, told as a detective investigation. Recommended style: Troubleshooting Decision Tree + Memory Palace hybrid.

Why this visual style for this session

Session 43 was not a topology session and not a packet-journey session — it was about reading evidence and branching on what it says. The detective/cold-case frame maps naturally onto can't-vs-won't, onto "the confession before the hypothesis", and onto a fault that sat in plain sight for months. Each placeholder below is a station in the investigation.

Station 4 · The Interrogation

Can't versus Won't

s43-visual-04-cant-vs-wont.png
The highest-value exam distinction of the session, given two distinct faces.
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), soft brown outlines, coral / gold / sage palette, navy ink. No gradients, no 3D. Composition: two illustrated doorways side by side, each set into a plain wall, viewed straight on. Both doors are CLOSED and identical in shape — this sameness is the point. LEFT DOORWAY: painted coral. A large plaque above reads "20". Below the plaque, in serif type: "CAN'T". The door has NO handle at all — smooth, handle-less, impossible by construction. Beside the frame, a small illustrated wall-mounted card holds a legible line of monospace-style text: "enc: aes-256-cbc auth: sha512", with "sha512" struck through in coral. A tiny etched circuit-board motif runs along the door's edge, suggesting silicon. RIGHT DOORWAY: painted sage green. Plaque above reads "00". Below it: "WON'T". This door HAS a handle, and beside it a wall switch in the OFF position, with a small hand-lettered sticky note hanging from it reading "back in 5 min" — yellowed and clearly old. A trail of footprints in the dust leads away from the switch and does not return. BETWEEN the two doors, a floor-standing sign in navy reads: "Same symptom. Different fix. Only one needs a signature." At the bottom edge, small and understated in serif: "00 = not yet. 03 = yes. 20 = never — and never has a reason." Lighting: flat and even, with one detail — a thin shaft of light under the sage door only, hinting that the room beyond is reachable. Must NOT appear: human figures, faces, brand logos, any keyboard or screen. Learning purpose: encodes the exam-critical pair as two physically different obstacles — a handle-less door (hardware refusal, permanent, needs sign-off) versus a switched-off door (human action, reversible, put it back). The dusty footprints reinforce that leftover troubleshooting state is the most common cause.
Aspect ratio
16:9 · Place beside the can't-vs-won't comparison table.
Accessibility description
Two identical closed doors: the coral "20 — Can't" door has no handle and a crossed-out sha512 card; the sage "00 — Won't" door has a handle and an off switch with an old "back in 5 min" note.
Reinforces
Identical symptoms, different owners. One is silicon; one is a person who never came back.
Station 5 · The Vault

Why the SA Copy Is Lazy

s43-visual-05-lazy-key-vault.png
The one mechanism the student derived unaided — worth cementing visually.
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), soft brown line work, coral / gold / sage palette, navy ink. Educational-diagram clarity with storybook warmth. Composition: a horizontal three-stage sequence, read left to right, with two coral arrows between stages. Each stage is a small illustrated vignette on its own subtle rounded panel. STAGE 1, labelled "PHASE 2 UP — npu_flag=00": a large open KERNEL storeroom drawn as a spacious warehouse shelf, holding two ornate keys side by side, one tagged "OUT" and one tagged "IN". To the right of the warehouse, a small, obviously CRAMPED safe labelled "NPU MEMORY" sits completely EMPTY, its door open. The safe is deliberately drawn much smaller than the warehouse — the size difference is the lesson. STAGE 2, labelled "FIRST PACKET — npu_flag=01": a single envelope-shaped packet travels along a track from left to right. The "OUT" key has been lifted from the warehouse and placed inside the small safe. The "IN" key remains on the warehouse shelf. A dotted line shows the key's journey. STAGE 3, labelled "BOTH DIRECTIONS — npu_flag=03": both keys now sit inside the small safe, which is now nearly full. A sage-green tick sits above it. The warehouse still holds copies, drawn faintly. Beneath the whole sequence, a slim horizontal caption bar in navy reads: "NPU memory is finite. Don't spend it on a tunnel that isn't carrying anything." Visual hierarchy: the dramatic size contrast between the roomy kernel warehouse and the tiny NPU safe must be the first thing the eye registers. Must NOT appear: human figures, brand logos, realistic server photographs, any binary or hexadecimal beyond the labels specified. Learning purpose: makes the lazy-copy rationale physical — scarcity explains the behaviour, and the 01→03 progression becomes a sequence of objects moving rather than a table to memorise.
Aspect ratio
16:9 · Place in the IPsec-on-the-NP section, before the npu_flag table.
Accessibility description
A three-stage sequence showing two keys moving from a large "kernel" warehouse into a much smaller "NPU memory" safe — empty at flag 00, one key at 01, both keys at 03.
Reinforces
The NPU is small. That scarcity is the whole reason the copy is deferred until traffic proves the tunnel is worth the space.
Station 6 · The Detective Wall

The Full Decision Tree

Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), bold soft-brown line work, coral string, muted gold pins, sage green accents, navy ink lettering. No gradients, no photorealism. Composition: a classic detective investigation wall, viewed straight on, filling the frame. From it, two strings descend to two branch cards: LEFT branch card: "TUNNEL → diagnose vpn tunnel list" RIGHT branch card: "SESSION → diagnose sys session list" Under the LEFT branch, four small cards fanned out, each with its string: · "03 → healthy. How to use these six

Day of study: look at Station 3 (filing cabinets) and Station 4 (two doors) only. Those two carry the material most likely to be lost.

Night before the exam: Station 6 alone. If you can redraw the branches from memory on paper, the session is banked.

When you next meet a slow tunnel at work: Station 1. It exists to make you go and look at something nobody has checked.
