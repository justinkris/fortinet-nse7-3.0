Title: Anchor image — establishes the emotional hook that makes the whole session memorable.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed illustrated artwork in a modern vector-art aesthetic on a warm cream off-white background (#faf5e9). Clean bold soft-brown line work, no gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D. Colour palette: coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage green, deep navy ink.

Composition: a three-quarter view of a corporate network room bench, camera at desk height, slightly low angle to give the object dignity. Centre frame: a single blue Ethernet patch lead draped over the corner of a monitor like a scarf, hanging in a gentle curve. A small handwritten paper luggage-tag label is tied to it with string, large enough to read, bearing the text "EL TÚNEL — 26 SESSIONS — NOBODY CHECKED". The tag is slightly curled and coffee-stained.

Around it: a chaotic but characterful desk — coffee cup with a ring stain, a red marker pen, loose cables coiled like sleeping animals. On the wall behind, immaculate hand-drawn network diagrams in navy ink, in deliberate contrast to the messy desk. In the mid-background, softly out of focus, a small potted cactus wearing tiny sunglasses sits on a shelf.

Lighting: warm morning light entering from the left, casting a long soft shadow from the patch lead across the bench, suggesting something that has been sitting untouched for a very long time. Fine dust motes in the light beam.

Mood: quiet, slightly melancholy, affectionate — an object that has been overlooked rather than broken. Visual hierarchy: the label text is the sharpest, highest-contrast element in the frame.

Must NOT appear: any human figures, any brand logos, any screens showing readable interface text, graphs, or charts. No text other than the label wording specified.

Learning purpose: creates a durable emotional anchor for the concept of a LATENT DEFECT — a fault that is present from build, causes no outage, and is therefore never investigated. Whenever the student later sees npu_flag=20, this image should surface.

Title: Targets the session's single most repeated error — fields migrating between tables in memory.

Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), bold soft-brown line work, coral / muted gold / sage green palette, navy ink text. Storybook-professional, educational clarity, generous spacing.

Composition: a tidy archive room, straight-on elevation view. TWO large filing cabinets stand side by side, clearly distinct in colour.

Two folders are lifted slightly and tabbed in coral, their tabs reading "offload = X / Y" and "ips_offload = X / Y". A small pictogram is pinned to this cabinet: a simple winding ROAD with a van on it, indicating "paths".

RIGHT CABINET, muted gold, brass plate reading "VPN TUNNEL LIST". Its drawer is also open, one folder tabbed in coral reading "npu_flag = NN". A pictogram pinned to this cabinet: a padlock and key, indicating "IPsec SAs".

BETWEEN the two cabinets, floating in mid-air and drawn with a dashed outline to show it is intangible, a vintage telephone handset with motion lines, labelled beneath in small serif type: "IPSA — no folder exists". Across the top of the image in elegant serif lettering: "What can this table physically record?"

Title: The highest-value exam distinction of the session, given two distinct faces.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), soft brown outlines, coral / gold / sage palette, navy ink. No gradients, no 3D.

Composition: two illustrated doorways side by side, each set into a plain wall, viewed straight on. Both doors are CLOSED and identical in shape — this sameness is the point.

LEFT DOORWAY: painted coral. A large plaque above reads "20". Below the plaque, in serif type: "CAN'T". The door has NO handle at all — smooth, handle-less, impossible by construction. Beside the frame, a small illustrated wall-mounted card holds a legible line of monospace-style text: "enc: aes-256-cbc   auth: sha512", with "sha512" struck through in coral. A tiny etched circuit-board motif runs along the door's edge, suggesting silicon.

RIGHT DOORWAY: painted sage green. Plaque above reads "00". Below it: "WON'T". This door HAS a handle, and beside it a wall switch in the OFF position, with a small hand-lettered sticky note hanging from it reading "back in 5 min" — yellowed and clearly old. A trail of footprints in the dust leads away from the switch and does not return.

BETWEEN the two doors, a floor-standing sign in navy reads: "Same symptom. Different fix. Only one needs a signature."

At the bottom edge, small and understated in serif: "00 = not yet. 03 = yes. 20 = never — and never has a reason."

Lighting: flat and even, with one detail — a thin shaft of light under the sage door only, hinting that the room beyond is reachable.

Must NOT appear: human figures, faces, brand logos, any keyboard or screen.

Learning purpose: encodes the exam-critical pair as two physically different obstacles — a handle-less door (hardware refusal, permanent, needs sign-off) versus a switched-off door (human action, reversible, put it back). The dusty footprints reinforce that leftover troubleshooting state is the most common cause.

Title: The one mechanism the student derived unaided — worth cementing visually.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), soft brown line work, coral / gold / sage palette, navy ink. Educational-diagram clarity with storybook warmth.

Composition: a horizontal three-stage sequence, read left to right, with two coral arrows between stages. Each stage is a small illustrated vignette on its own subtle rounded panel.

STAGE 1, labelled "PHASE 2 UP — npu_flag=00": a large open KERNEL storeroom drawn as a spacious warehouse shelf, holding two ornate keys side by side, one tagged "OUT" and one tagged "IN". To the right of the warehouse, a small, obviously CRAMPED safe labelled "NPU MEMORY" sits completely EMPTY, its door open. The safe is deliberately drawn much smaller than the warehouse — the size difference is the lesson.

STAGE 2, labelled "FIRST PACKET — npu_flag=01": a single envelope-shaped packet travels along a track from left to right. The "OUT" key has been lifted from the warehouse and placed inside the small safe. The "IN" key remains on the warehouse shelf. A dotted line shows the key's journey.

STAGE 3, labelled "BOTH DIRECTIONS — npu_flag=03": both keys now sit inside the small safe, which is now nearly full. A sage-green tick sits above it. The warehouse still holds copies, drawn faintly.

Beneath the whole sequence, a slim horizontal caption bar in navy reads: "NPU memory is finite. Don't spend it on a tunnel that isn't carrying anything."

Visual hierarchy: the dramatic size contrast between the roomy kernel warehouse and the tiny NPU safe must be the first thing the eye registers.

Must NOT appear: human figures, brand logos, realistic server photographs, any binary or hexadecimal beyond the labels specified.

Learning purpose: makes the lazy-copy rationale physical — scarcity explains the behaviour, and the 01→03 progression becomes a sequence of objects moving rather than a table to memorise.

Title: The recall image — the one to look at the morning of the exam.

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), bold soft-brown line work, coral string, muted gold pins, sage green accents, navy ink lettering. No gradients, no photorealism.

Composition: a classic detective investigation wall, viewed straight on, filling the frame. Index cards and printouts pinned to a large corkboard, connected by taut coral string running between gold pins. The layout must read clearly as a TOP-DOWN BRANCHING TREE, not a random collage.

TOP CARD (largest, centred): "SLOWER THAN THE DESIGN PREDICTS".

From it, two strings descend to two branch cards:
LEFT branch card: "TUNNEL → diagnose vpn tunnel list"
RIGHT branch card: "SESSION → diagnose sys session list"

Under the LEFT branch, four small cards fanned out, each with its string:
· "03 → healthy. Look elsewhere." (sage tick)
· "20 → CAN'T. Read enc: / auth:" (coral)
· "00 + traffic → WON'T. Check npu-offload" (coral)
· "00 + no traffic → not a fault" (grey, deliberately dimmed)

Under the RIGHT branch, three small cards:
· "no_ofld_reason → read the confession"
· "offload=9/0 → return path suspect"
· "ips_offload=0/0 → NTurbo not active"

At the BOTTOM of the board, separated by a horizontal coral string, one wider card in a different colour (muted gold) reading: "FIXED IT? Now ask what removed the headroom."

Pinned in the lower right corner, small and slightly askew, a photograph-style card showing the patch lead with the "EL TÚNEL" tag from Station 1, tying the investigation back to the case.

Lighting: even and flat, board fully legible. Visual hierarchy: the top card is largest, branch cards medium, leaf cards small but fully readable.

Must NOT appear: human figures, faces, brand logos, blood or crime-scene imagery, any text beyond the card wording specified.

Learning purpose: converts the written decision tree into a single spatial image the student can recall wholesale under exam pressure. The gold bottom card ensures the root-cause step is remembered as part of the method rather than an optional extra.
