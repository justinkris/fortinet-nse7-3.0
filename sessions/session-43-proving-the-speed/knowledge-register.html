GROWTH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------------
1. *** CLAUSE COLLAPSE (NEW #1 PRIORITY — supersedes the S41/S42
   "consolidation gap" framing, which was diagnosed too broadly).
   SIX of ten gauntlet answers were half-marks, and in EVERY ONE
   the first clause was right and the second was absent. Four of
   those six covered material he had produced correctly WITHIN
   THE HOUR. This is not decay and not a knowledge gap — it is a
   STOPPING habit. He answers the first thing asked and stops.
   COUNTERMEASURE (concrete, prescribed to him in-session):
   count the question marks in the question; two questions = two
   sentences. Enforce mechanically in S44 — refuse to grade any
   multi-clause answer that arrives as one clause, hand it back
   once, and record whether the second pass is complete.
   This one habit is worth ~3 marks per gauntlet.
2. TABLE-CONFUSION (new, see entry above): fields migrate between
   the session list and the tunnel list in his memory. Needs
   vocabulary drilling, not reasoning.
3. GUESS-WITH-A-QUESTION-MARK: "NP7, 8?" on Q6. He knows the
   difference between knowing and guessing (Q5 proves it) but
   doesn't apply it consistently. Coach: a hedged wrong number
   scores the same as a wrong number; "not sure" costs the same
   and preserves the diagnostic signal.
4. FIX-BEFORE-DIAGNOSIS: repeatedly produced the correct remedial
   command while unable to state the fault as a sentence. Harmless
   in a lab, dangerous in a change window, and marked wrong on an
   exam that asks "what is the cause."
5. DECLINED A REASONING QUESTION ("Lets move on") for the first
   time, on SHA-256 vs SHA-512 security. Single instance, 90 min
   in. Not yet a pattern. WATCH on the next crypto-adjacent topic.
