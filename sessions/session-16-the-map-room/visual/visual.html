The Map Room — Seeing the LSDB

OSPF internals I: the map, the maths, the trees, and the boundaries

Recommended Visual Style

Memory Palace: "The Map Room" itself — one physical room in which each object anchors one concept. This session was literally staged in a room around a hand-drawn map, so the strongest long-term memory strategy is to make that room the palace: the wall map (LSDB), witnesses handing in testimony slips (LSAs), a toll-road model on the table (cost + Dijkstra), five easels with different trees drawn from one map, and a glass wall dividing the room (areas). A secondary Real-World Analogy visual covers the asymmetric-cost firewall trap, and a Process Flow visual covers reading the database output.

Walk the room, station by station

Each placeholder below is one station of the palace. Revisit them in order; each image, once generated, should trigger the full mental model attached to it.

Station 3 — The Toll-Road Table (cost, exit tolls, Dijkstra)
Aspect ratio 16:9 · Place beside the worked cost example
Purpose

Anchor: path cost = sum of EXIT tolls; Dijkstra compares all candidate paths and keeps the cheapest; via-R3 (1+2=3) beats via-R4 (3+1+2=6).

Image Generation Prompt
Flat detailed vector illustration, cream background, coral/gold/sage palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients. A tabletop model-railway-style miniature landscape on a wooden table in the Map Room, viewed from a slightly elevated three-quarter angle. Five miniature router buildings labelled R1–R5 connected by toy roads representing network segments Net 1–Net 5. Each road has a small toll gate AT ITS ENTRY POINT on each end, with a gold coin icon and a red number showing that end's exit cost (Net 3 gates: 1 and 1; Net 2 gates: 3 and 3; Net 5 gates: 2 and 2; Net 4 gates: 1 and 1). Two toy delivery vans depart from R2 heading for Net 5: one van on the short route through R3 with a floating tally "1 + 2 = 3" in sage green with a laurel/checkmark; the second van on the long route through R4 with tally "3 + 1 + 2 = 6" in muted coral, drooping slightly. Above the table, a small stylised tree diagram grows out of R2's rooftop like a plant — the SPF tree — with Net 4 and Net 5 hanging from a branch that passes through a tiny R3 icon. Señor's hand (with sleeve cuff) reaches in from frame edge placing the winning van's route flag. No photorealism, no dense labels beyond the tallies and R/Net names. Mood: playful precision. Accessibility description: a miniature toll-road model where two delivery vans race from R2 to Net 5, the cheaper route (total 3) winning over the expensive one (total 6), while a small tree diagram sprouts from R2.

Station 4 — The Two-Bridge Trap (asymmetric cost vs the stateful firewall)
Aspect ratio 3:2 · Place beside "The Asymmetric Cost Trap" — the student-requested scenario
Purpose

Anchor: cost is paid on exit and is directional; a one-sided cost tweak sends the SYN over one bridge and the SYN-ACK over another, where a stateful guard with no record of the conversation drops it. "Symmetry is your job, not OSPF's."

Image Generation Prompt
Flat detailed vector illustration, cream background, coral/gold/sage palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, storybook-professional. A river landscape with TWO parallel bridges (North Bridge and South Bridge) connecting a client-side bank (small office building, a person at a laptop) to a server-side bank (small server rack building). Each bridge has a guard hut at its midpoint staffed by a stern flat-design security-guard character holding a ledger — these are stateful firewalls. Each bridge end has a small toll sign with a red number; the numbers are visibly DIFFERENT on the two directions (e.g., North: 10 outbound side, 15 return side; South: 15 outbound, 10 return). A friendly envelope character labelled "SYN" walks left-to-right across the NORTH bridge; the North guard writes it into his ledger, page glowing softly gold. A second envelope labelled "SYN-ACK" walks right-to-left across the SOUTH bridge — where the South guard, ledger visibly EMPTY, blocks it with an outstretched palm and a coral X above the envelope's head; the envelope looks bewildered. Above the whole scene a small banner ribbon reads "every routing table technically correct" in understated lettering. In a lower corner, a tiny Señor figure in sombrero writes on a signpost: "Cost is paid on exit." No photorealism, minimal text beyond the two labels and banner. Mood: gentle tragedy, cautionary fable. Accessibility description: two toll bridges with different directional prices; a SYN envelope crosses the north bridge and is logged, but its SYN-ACK reply returns via the south bridge where an unaware guard blocks it.

How to revise with this page

Walk the palace in order and say each anchor aloud before reading it: the wall map (what the LSDB is) → the witness stand (how it's built) → the toll table (how it's priced and computed) → the two bridges (the directional trap) → the five easels (map vs trees, loops) → the glass wall (areas and blast radius) → the proof (reading it on the CLI). If any station fails to trigger its idea, that's the section to reopen in the complete guide.
