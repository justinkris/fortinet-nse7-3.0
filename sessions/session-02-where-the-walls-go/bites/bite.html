SESSION 02 · BITE · FIREWALL PLACEMENT — 5 MINUTES

Three Addresses, One Job Description

DEFW · ISFW · DCFW are places. NGFW is a role. Read this before work; know it before the exam.

MINUTE 1

Two jobs, not one

Edge inspection ≠ internal inspection. The edge faces the internet — its enemy is hostile content, so it runs deep inspection (web filter, app control, IPS: NGFW duties). Internal points face your own network — their enemy is lateral movement, so they run least-privilege segmentation at very low latency.

Junior trap: "enable everything everywhere." Every engine costs time; at a trading firm, milliseconds are money. Inspection posture is chosen per placement.

MINUTE 4

East-west dwarfs north-south

One order leaves the building; behind it, market-data fan-out ×42, DB replication and backups churn inside the data centre — often 10× or more the perimeter volume. That's why the DCFW needs hyperscale throughput and a realistic inspection posture (hardware-accelerated segmentation via VRFs/VDOMs/zones), while deep inspection of traffic entering the DC usually lives on an NGFW gateway in front. A DCFW can face the internet directly, carved up with VDOMs.

MINUTE 5

The exam's favourite trap

Role ≠ model. Enable IPS + web filtering on a campus 100–900 ISFW and it is now functioning as an NGFW — same box, same invoice. Real networks are a hybrid mesh: mixed models, mixed roles, one FortiOS, one FortiManager.

Branch scenario: backhauled branch → protection happens at HQ; DEFW needs no local UTM. Junior is right ONLY with local internet breakout — and then you likely need a bigger box.
