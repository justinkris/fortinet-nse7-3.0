BUSINESS PROBLEM
------------------------------------------------------------------
Custody dispute over the Security Rating output between two
stakeholders with legitimate but conflicting needs (visibility vs.

audience rather than picking a side, and by closing cheap findings
before the deadline so the presented number is itself improved,
not just contextualised.

TECHNICAL CONCEPTS COVERED
------------------------------------------------------------------
1. SECURITY RATING ARCHITECTURE: root compiles a fabric-wide audit;
   each member FortiGate self-checks its own config against
   best-practice checks and reports results upward. Same
   distribute-work/centralise-verdict pattern as stitches (config
   authored on root, executed locally) and fabric logging (each
   member logs independently to FAZ).
2. RATING RE-RUNS AUTOMATICALLY on a schedule (not just on click) —
   explains Riley's "why did this change with nobody touching it"
   ticket. Can ALSO be triggered on-demand from the GUI — used to
   force a fresh score after Mon/Tue remediation, ahead of the
   scheduled cycle.
3. SCORE ANATOMY: weighted points per check (severity ×
   device-importance) → letter grade. TWO context layers turn a
   floating number into board-usable information: (a) TREND — score
   over time, because the rating has been running since fabric
   go-live; (b) INDUSTRY PERCENTILE — compared to similar-sized
   organizations in the same sector. Both the full check set and
   percentile comparison require a FortiGuard Security Rating
   subscription; without it, checks and comparison degrade/vanish
   while local score/findings largely still compute — same
   asymmetric-degradation pattern as S23's FortiGuard web-filter
   outage. Diagnostic instinct reused correctly: `diagnose
   autoupdate versions` as the paperwork check.
4. FINDINGS TRIAGE — THREE LENSES (beyond raw severity order):
   (a) EXPOSURE — who can reach it unprompted? internet / internal /
   post-compromise-only. (b) BLAST RADIUS — what does a successful
   exploit hand the attacker? (c) COST TO FIX — zero-disruption CLI
   change vs. requires a change window (e.g., firmware = reboot =
   plan around trading hours). Some findings offer a one-click
   remediation shortcut directly from the Rating page ("free wins").
5. EXAMPLE FINDING SET WORKED: critical = admin HTTPS enabled on
   WAN interface (precondition-free, internet-facing — categorically
   worse than anything requiring prior compromise); high = no
   trusted hosts on admin accounts (cheap, high-value insurance
   against credential/lateral-movement risk); medium = stale
   firmware on a branch box (schedule for change window, reboot
   risk); medium = "all/all" firewall policies (cannot be tightened
   safely from config alone — must audit actual traffic via FAZ
   logs, over a WIDE window to catch rare/periodic flows before
   assuming a service is unused); low = admin password policy not
   enforced (cheap, hour-one fix).
6. CONFIG-BACKUP STITCH PROOF POINT: Schedule-triggered backups land
   in Configuration > Revisions — the version-history page, also
   used for rollback. Hook: "Schedule trigger → Revisions page."
7. SCHEDULE TRIGGER FREQUENCIES (corrected this session): Once,
   Hourly, Daily, Weekly, Monthly — NOT "Now." Governed by the
   ROOT FortiGate's system clock, because the stitch is authored on
   the root and pushed to leaves ("root's ink, root's clock"). This
   is a repeat-miss item (also missed cold in S38) — now flagged for
   an unprompted cold ambush in a future session rather than another
   warm-up.
8. MANUAL QUARANTINE CLI (retained from S38, applied live):
   `diagnose endpoint fctems queue-complete-calls
   Q-<endpoint_ip_address>` — manually injects the same instruction
   EMS would send after an automated IoC detection. Not supported on
   FortiClient (Linux) — reconfirmed cold.
9. SAML SSO ROLES (retest): root = sole IdP; downstream FortiGates,
   FortiAnalyzer, FortiManager = SPs. FortiAuthenticator is NOT part
   of this fabric SSO picture — it's a separate standalone IdP
   product; naming it as an SP was a planted distractor and was
   taken. "One fabric, one anchor, one voucher" derivation still
   solid; vendor-name-similarity traps remain a live risk pattern.
10. FMG REINSTALL-KEEPS-MAPPING: cold retest passed a third
    consecutive time (decayed S37, re-anchored, held S38, held
    again S39) — recommend ONE final confirmation then retire from
    active spaced-retest queue.
11. TRIPTYCH RELATIONSHIP (Watching/Acting/Judging) — INTRODUCED,
    NOT YET DEMONSTRATED BY STUDENT: Watching (FAZ, memory) and
    Acting (stitches, reflexes) must exist BEFORE Judging (Rating)
    is useful, because a score alone doesn't fix anything — FAZ
    supplies evidence to act correctly on findings (e.g., the
    ISFW policy example), and stitches give the fabric a way to
    respond to what Judging keeps finding. This is an OPEN LOOP —
    student answered a related-but-different question (IoC chain

TROUBLESHOOTING / DESIGN APPROACH TAUGHT
------------------------------------------------------------------
Referee framing: read what the artefact actually IS before ruling
on who gets it. Split deliverables by AUDIENCE and PURPOSE rather
than picking a side in a two-stakeholder conflict — Penny needed
"is it improving / how do we compare," Cipher needed "protect the
target package," and both needs were served by separating derived
metrics from raw findings. Triage findings by exposure → blast
radius → cost-to-fix, not raw severity alone. Never guess-tighten a
firewall policy from config alone — pull FAZ evidence first, over a
window wide enough to catch periodic/rare legitimate flows.
