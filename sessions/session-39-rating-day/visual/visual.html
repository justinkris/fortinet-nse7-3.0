Rating Day — Seeing the Referee's Ruling

Recommended style: Troubleshooting Decision Tree, blended with a Real-World Analogy panel for the score architecture.

Style recommendation for this session: the core teaching moment was a TRIAGE DECISION process (exposure → blast radius → cost-to-fix), so the primary visual is a Decision-Tree / Detective-Board hybrid. A second, smaller illustration covers the self-audit/root-compile architecture as a Real-World Analogy (marking your own homework). Two placeholders total — enough to anchor both concepts without diluting focus.
1. The Referee's Findings Board
Purpose: memory anchor for the exposure → blast-radius → cost-to-fix triage lens, worked against the five actual LoFinance findings.
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER — 16:9
Aspect ratio: 16:9Placement: inside "Findings Triage" section
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic vector-art illustration, modern educational diagram style, cream/warm off-white background (#faf5e9), coral accent color, muted gold highlights, sage green secondary accents, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D rendering, generous spacing, storybook-professional aesthetic. Composition: a wide corkboard mounted on an office wall, viewed straight-on, styled like a detective's investigation board but calm and organized rather than frantic. Five index cards are pinned to the board in a horizontal row, each connected by a soft coral string to one of three labeled overhead category zones: "EXPOSURE" (a stylized globe icon, simple line art, top-left zone), "BLAST RADIUS" (a stylized concentric-circle/ripple icon, top-middle zone), and "COST TO FIX" (a stylized calendar-with-clock icon, top-right zone). Each of the five index cards has a small icon suggesting its content without dense text: a padlock-on-a-cloud icon (WAN admin access finding) connects with a coral string to the EXPOSURE zone and is visually the largest/most prominent card, subtly glowing or bordered in coral to signal highest priority; a house-key icon (trusted hosts finding) connects to the COST TO FIX zone with a small green checkmark badge signaling "free win"; a small gear-with-wrench icon (firmware finding) connects to COST TO FIX zone with a small calendar badge signaling "scheduled, not urgent"; a wide-open door icon (broad "all/all" policy finding) connects to BLAST RADIUS zone with a small magnifying-glass badge signaling "needs evidence"; a small padlock-with-dot icon (password policy) connects to COST TO FIX zone with a green checkmark badge. A single stylized figure — a calm, competent-looking engineer in business-casual clothing (rolled sleeves, simple silhouette, gender-neutral, no specific facial detail beyond a thoughtful expression), rendered in the same flat vector style — stands to one side, one hand raised toward the board as if organizing it, embodying the "referee" role from the lesson. No sombrero on this figure (that belongs to a different character in the wider story). The overall mood is methodical and clear-headed, not urgent or alarming — this is expertise being visualized, not a crisis. Do not include: dense blocks of readable text, any real company logos, photorealistic rendering, red flashing alarm imagery, gore or violence of any kind, any real identifiable people. Learning purpose: reinforce that findings triage sorts by THREE criteria simultaneously (exposure, blast radius, cost-to-fix) rather than by severity label alone, and that different findings warrant different response speeds even when nominally similar in severity.
Accessibility description: An organized corkboard illustration showing five security-finding cards connected by strings to three category headers reading Exposure, Blast Radius, and Cost to Fix, with a calm engineer figure organizing the board.
2. Marking Your Own Homework
Purpose: memory anchor for the Security Rating's self-audit-then-compile architecture (each fabric member checks itself; root compiles the unified score).
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER — 3:2
Aspect ratio: 3:2Placement: inside "How the Rating Works" section
Image Generation Prompt
Flat minimalistic vector-art illustration, modern educational diagram style, cream/warm off-white background (#faf5e9), coral accent color, muted gold highlights, sage green secondary accents, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism, generous spacing, storybook-professional aesthetic, consistent with a companion image using the same palette and character style. Composition: a simple tree-shaped diagram rendered as a friendly illustrated network topology, NOT a dense technical stencil diagram. At the top, a single larger stylized firewall-device icon (a simple rounded rectangle with a small shield glyph on its face) labeled implicitly as the "root" through visual prominence (slightly larger, positioned at the apex, softly glowing with a muted gold outline). Below it, three smaller identical firewall-device icons in a row, each connected upward to the root by a simple coral line, representing downstream fabric members. Each of the three smaller devices has a tiny illustrated clipboard-and-checkmark icon floating just above it, symbolizing "self-audit" — as if each device is quietly reviewing its own homework. Small upward-pointing sage-green arrows travel from each clipboard icon along the connecting lines toward the root device, visually showing results flowing UP the tree. At the root device, the three incoming arrows merge into a single stylized document icon with a letter grade badge (a simple rounded badge showing an abstract "grade" shape, not real readable text) appearing to emerge from the root, symbolizing the compiled unified score. Include a small decorative visual metaphor beside the diagram: a simplified illustration of a classroom scene rendered in the same flat style — a few small stylized "students" (simple geometric figures, no specific faces) each looking down at their own small paper on a desk, while a single "teacher" figure at the front holds one combined stack of papers, visually rhyming with the fabric diagram beside it, reinforcing the "marking your own homework, handed up to one compiler" analogy without any readable text. Do not include: real Fortinet product logos or trademarked iconography, dense technical labels, readable text of any kind, any real identifiable people, glossy or photorealistic rendering. Learning purpose: reinforce that the Security Rating is NOT the root reaching out and inspecting every device — it is each device self-checking locally, with only the RESULTS flowing upward to be compiled into one unified score at the root. This distinguishes the rating's architecture from a common but incorrect mental model (root actively scanning everyone).
Accessibility description: A simple tree diagram showing one larger root firewall device with three smaller downstream devices beneath it, each performing a self-check with results flowing upward to merge into one compiled score at the root, alongside a small classroom illustration of students each reviewing their own paper.
