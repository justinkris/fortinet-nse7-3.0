Rating Day

Security Fabric hardening — Security Rating, findings triage, verification

How the Rating Works

ARCHITECTURE Each fabric member self-audits its own config against best-practice checks. The root compiles all results into one score/grade.

Pattern: distribute the work, centralise the verdict — same as stitches and logging.

Turning a Score Into a Decision Tool

  • Trend — the rating auto-reruns on a schedule, so a history exists.
  • Industry percentile — compared to similar-sized orgs. Both trend depth and percentile need a FortiGuard Security Rating subscription.
  • Can also be triggered on demand from the GUI — don't wait on the schedule before a deadline.

Triage: Three Lenses, Not Just Severity

  • Exposure — who can attempt it with zero precondition? (internet-facing = worst)
  • Blast radius — what does a win hand the attacker?
  • Cost to fix — instant CLI change vs. needs a change window (e.g. firmware = reboot).
"Severity tells you how bad the fall is; exposure tells you how many people are near the ledge."

Evidence Before Action

Never guess-tighten an "all/all" policy from config alone. Pull FortiAnalyzer traffic logs first — over a window wide enough to catch rare/periodic flows (month-end jobs, weekly backups).

Schedule Trigger — Frequencies + Clock

Once, Hourly, Daily, Weekly, Monthly — no "Now" option.

Governed by the root's system time (stitches are authored on root, pushed to leaves).

Verification Points

  • Config backups land in Configuration > Revisions.
  • Manual quarantine: diagnose endpoint fctems queue-complete-calls Q-<ip> — not supported on FortiClient (Linux).
  • License check: diagnose autoupdate versions.
