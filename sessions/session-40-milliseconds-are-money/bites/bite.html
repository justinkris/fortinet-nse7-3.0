Milliseconds Are Money: SPU Architecture

NP, CP, SoC, and the architecture that removes the toll booth.

The One Cut

Two Kinds of Work, Two Kinds of Chip

Sort firewall workloads by the shape of the problem: repetitive per-packet forwarding (decided-once — like a switch ASIC's job) vs content work (searching inside payloads). Fortinet's ASICs — SPUs — mirror the cut exactly.

NP at the ports. CP beside the CPU. Plain IPsec → NP ("a forwarding job in a trench coat"). SSL-heavy workload on an NP-only box → CPU meltdown; that crypto is CP work.

The Number

12 Million Sessions — Per Chip

NP7 holds up to 12M sessions, limited by processor memory. Session 12,000,001 overflows to the CPU (slow path). Ports are wired to specific NP7s and tables don't pool — distribute traffic across chips or one NP7 overflows while its sibling sits idle. Cabling is capacity planning.

The Architecture

NP Direct — Remove the ISF

The ISF (internal switch fabric) glues every port to every processor — and charges a latency toll on every packet, twice. NP Direct wires interfaces straight into the NP, eliminating the ISF → lowest-latency forwarding.

  • NP6: boxes with 2+ NP6 chips — 200E, 2000E, 2500E.
  • NP7: ultralow-latency interfaces — 400F, 600F, 900G (ports X5–X8 direct to NP7).
The rule: offloaded traffic must enter AND exit on interfaces of the same NP. "Same chip in, same chip out — there is no bridge anymore."
The trade: ISF = flexibility (any port, any processor). NP Direct gives it up for latency — which is why NP6 boxes need 2+ chips to afford it. Physical topology becomes part of the offload design.
Verification

Two Commands

# diagnose hardware lspci | grep 1a29 Device 1a29:4e36 → NP6 Device 1a29:4e37 → NP7 # get hardware status ASIC version: CP9 → CP generation (discrete models) ASIC version: SOC5 → confirms SP5 combined chip

1a29 = Fortinet's PCI vendor ID. Verify the rack before trusting a quote.

Don't Conflate

Two Different Latency Diagnoses

ISF-hop latency (architectural — fix: NP Direct) ≠ CPU-overflow latency (capacity — fix: distribute sessions across NPs). Different faults, different fixes; distractors bet you'll merge them.
