RECOMMENDED VISUAL STYLE

Packet Journey, Built on a Road

Chosen approach · Packet Journey + Real-World Analogy

Three styles were candidates for this session. Enterprise Topology would have shown the two links accurately but flattened the idea — the whole point is that the topology looks identical before and after, and a topology diagram cannot show “identical, but one of them is lying.” Layer-by-Layer would have handled the four-rung ladder but lost the story.

Packet Journey was chosen because it matches how you actually reasoned this session. You did not learn this material by listing attributes — you learned it by tracing: what does the route entry contain, what happens to a probe, what gates does a member clear. Every question you answered strongly was a trace. Every question you missed was a list. The visual should follow the trace.

It is blended with Real-World Analogy because the two images that unlocked the session — Riley’s clipboard at the fork, and the thermometer that does not drive anyone to hospital — are both analogies, and both deserve to be seen rather than remembered as sentences.

🧠
MENTAL NOTE
You trace; you do not list. Build every memory aid as a path with checkpoints, never as a table of attributes.

USING THIS PAGE

How to Revise From the Pictures

Do not read the panels. Walk them. Start at the fork and narrate the packet’s journey out loud, one panel at a time, without looking at the captions. The test is whether each image produces the sentence rather than the sentence producing the image.

  • Panel 1 should produce: “both up, one lying, nothing can tell them apart.”
  • Panel 2 should produce: “no field for latency.”
  • Panel 3 should produce: “latency, jitter, loss — TCP stalls, UDP gaps.”
  • Panel 4 should produce: “the SLA measures, the rule acts.”
  • Panel 5 should produce: “up, eligible, preferred — and out-of-SLA only moves the last two.”
  • Panel 6 should produce: “interface and gateway required, zone optional, policies never move.”
  • Panel 7 should produce: “eight breakouts, eight identities.”
If a panel produces nothing, that is the section of the complete guide to reread — not the whole session. Seven panels, seven precise diagnostics.
