In the left third, a navy rounded-rectangle firewall labelled SYD-EDGE with the LoFinance ice-cube-and-banknotes logo on its face. From it, a single sage-green road leaves and immediately forks into two roads of visually IDENTICAL width, colour, surface and signage, running right toward a distant coral internet cloud. Above each road, a matching muted-gold road sign; both signs read exactly the same thing: STATUS: UP, with a small green tick. This identical-ness is the point of the image and must be unmistakable. Standing at the fork, a small anthropomorphised data packet character — a rounded sage-green square with simple dot eyes and short legs — looking left and right with a visible question mark above it. On the far right of the frame, beyond where the picture can see, the LOWER road is subtly drawn with faint heat-shimmer wavy lines and two tiny outlined packets tumbling off its edge, but this detail is small and set well past the fork, so that from the packet's viewpoint it is invisible. In the foreground bottom-left, Baz the carrier rep in a slightly-too-shiny jacket holds up a laminated coverage map showing two green dots, smiling, confident. Opposite him, Penny holds a small handwritten list with five tally entries and a puzzled expression. Between them, small navy caption text: BOTH ARE UP. ONE IS LYING. Do NOT include: any measurement gauges, thermometers, dashboards or metrics in this panel — the absence of measurement is what the panel is about. No CLI text, no screenshots, no 3D.
