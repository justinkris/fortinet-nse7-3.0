==================================================================
1. **THE MOST-RECENT-TOPIC DISTRACTOR.** THIRD CONSECUTIVE SEASON-LEVEL INSTANCE. Under
   multiple-choice pressure, whatever was most recently and vividly taught wins over what is
   structurally correct. S59: strongest memories as distractors. S60: elimination method
   rewarding familiarity. S62 Q2: "zone" had just had a whole teaching block, so it beat
   "gateway". THE S60 REMEDY ("score first, count last") WAS NOT VISIBLY APPLIED THIS SESSION.
   → It is now confirmed as an EXAM-TECHNIQUE problem, not a knowledge problem. Escalate.
2. **ARITHMETIC AVOIDANCE.** He reasons and does not compute. Two numbers in a question do not
   trigger a multiply. Cheap to fix, expensive if unfixed on a scenario exam.
3. **OPEN-ENDED ENUMERATION.** "List the pros and cons" underperforms badly against "here is
   Tuesday morning, what breaks". CONFIRMED AGAIN (DIA question). This is the single most
   reliable finding in this register across five sessions.
4. **SENSOR vs ACTUATOR CONFLATION** (new this session). Watch it in S63/S64.

LEARNING NOTES — how this student learns best
==================================================================
- **HE TRACES; HE DOES NOT LIST.** Fifth session of confirming this. Every failure this session
  was an enumeration failure (Q2 field list, DIA pros/cons). Every success was a trace (route
  contents, symptom→mechanism, probe→metric). DESIGN EVERY QUESTION AS A WIRE TO FOLLOW.
- **DERIVE-DON'T-DELIVER WORKS SPECTACULARLY.** The three SLA metrics were the best-learned
  content of the session and they were the ONLY content he was made to generate himself from
  two ticket descriptions. Compare: the member field list, delivered as config, missed on the
  exam ninety minutes later. THE CONTRAST IS STARK AND SHOULD DRIVE S63'S DESIGN.
- **ANALOGY RESCUES HIM WHERE REPETITION DOES NOT.** Two direct attempts to get "SD-WAN rule"
  failed. The thermometer worked immediately. WHEN HE CIRCLES A WORD TWICE, DO NOT ASK A THIRD
  TIME — CHANGE THE MODALITY.
- **NAMING HIS COGNITIVE MOVE STILL RAISES HIS GAME** (confirmed from S60). Telling him "you
  reached for the contents of the data structure" visibly sharpened the next four answers.
  Continue. This is the single cheapest intervention available.
- **HE RESPONDS TO BEING GIVEN THE WORD ONCE HE HAS EARNED IT.** Withholding "SD-WAN rule" for
  three exchanges built the concept; withholding it for a fourth would have cost momentum.
  Judge the handover point by whether he has produced the FUNCTION — if he can describe what
  the thing does, give him the name immediately.
- CONSOLIDATION (the S60 growth priority) SHOWED MEASURABLE IMPROVEMENT: 8/10 vs 7/10, and both
  misses were shallow (one field-list, one multiply) rather than conceptual. He did NOT,
  however, write anything down in his own words. The three-touch rule was not exercised because
  no correction needed three touches. Carry the priority forward but DOWNGRADE ITS URGENCY.

RECOMMENDED TEACHING ADJUSTMENTS FOR SESSION 63 ("Rules of the Road")
==================================================================
- COLD RETESTS AT THE OPEN, in this order, before any story:
    1. "What two things must a member have?" (Q2 miss — WIRE FORM: "FortiGate builds the
       member's route for you. What do you have to give it?")
    2. "Baz's link is out-of-SLA. Where is it still present?" (should be instant — confidence
       builder, and it is the load-bearing model for the whole rules lesson)
    3. failtime 10, interval 1000 — how many seconds? (ARITHMETIC. Make him multiply.)
    4. "Up, eligible, preferred — which one does the SLA change?"
- **THE IMPLICIT RULE IS THE PERFECT VEHICLE FOR ELIGIBILITY.** Build the whole eligibility
  concept on it rather than teaching it abstractly.
- **MAKE HIM DERIVE THE MATCHING CRITERIA** from J.R.'s catastrophic 0.0.0.0/0 rule rather than
  listing them. Ask: "J.R.'s rule matched everything. Name the knobs he SHOULD have turned."
  He will trace from the symptom. He will not recall a list.
- **ESCALATE THE EXAM-TECHNIQUE FIX.** Third season-consecutive instance of the recent-topic
  distractor. Before the S63 exam questions, state the rule explicitly and make him confirm it:
  "Score every option against the question before eliminating any. Familiarity is not evidence."
  Then set a deliberate trap where the most-recently-taught term is a wrong answer, and debrief
  it whether he falls in or not.
- ONE NUMERIC QUESTION MINIMUM. Non-negotiable now.
- HINT LEVEL: LOW for routing-table-vs-quality, out-of-SLA behaviour, zones, symptom→mechanism.
  MEDIUM for rule lookup order, matching criteria, local-out traffic.
  HIGH for member required fields (retest first), timer arithmetic, DIA return-path.
- DELIVER THE PROMISED JUNIOR MOMENT. The S62 teaser has Junior teaching J.R. about the
  implicit rule with genuine authority. This is four sessions of arc paying off. DO NOT DROP IT.
- Consider naming the provenance-honesty habit to him directly, once, in S63. He has earned it
  and naming his moves demonstrably raises his ambition.
