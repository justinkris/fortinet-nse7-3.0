SESSION 55 · VISUAL LEARNING · PACKET JOURNEY

The Journey of One SYN Packet

Six illustrations that turn the BGP state machine into a route you can walk in your head.

Recommended style for this session: Packet Journey, with a decision-tree spine.

The session's core insight is that a peering fails at a specific place along a path — and the state name tells you where. A journey visual encodes position, which is exactly the thing the FSM asks you to remember. A layer-by-layer breakdown would have shown the same facts without the geography, and the geography is the lesson.

VISUAL 01

The Journey — one SYN, six gates

Purpose: The spine of the whole session. Shows a single TCP SYN packet attempting the full path from FortiGate to ISP peer, with every checkpoint that can stop it, so the student can locate a failure spatially rather than recalling a list.

At the FAR RIGHT, a second appliance labelled 'ISP-1 Parramatta · AS 65100'. TRAVELLING ALONG THE ROAD: a single small character representing a TCP SYN packet — a rounded envelope with simple eyes and tiny legs, carrying a small suitcase labelled '179'. SIX GATES stand along the road, each drawn as a distinct illustrated barrier with a hand-lettered sign: GATE 1 'IS THERE A ROUTE?' drawn as a signpost with a folded map pinned to it; GATE 2 'RIGHT SOURCE ADDRESS?' drawn as a passport desk with a small stamp; GATE 3 'LOCAL-IN POLICY' drawn as a heavy closed portcullis in coral, clearly SHUT, with the SYN character bumping into it, one arm raised, expression startled — this gate must be visually the most prominent because it is the episode's actual fault; GATE 4 'TTL — CAN YOU SURVIVE ONE HOP?' drawn as a low tunnel with a height-limit bar and a small ticking hourglass; GATE 5 'UPSTREAM ACL' drawn as an open boom gate in sage green with a bored attendant; GATE 6 'DO THEY ANSWER?' drawn as a doorway with a bell. Beside GATE 3, a small inset speech bubble reads 'to the box, not through it'. A faint ghosted continuation of the road past the ISP router leads to a small banner reading 'ESTABLISHED' in muted gold, unreachable in this image. BOTTOM LEFT CORNER: the LoFinance logo. MUST NOT INCLUDE: configuration syntax, dense labels beyond those listed, photorealism, corporate stock art. LEARNING PURPOSE: convert an abstract list of failure causes into an ordered spatial walk, so the student checks causes in path order instead of at random.
Aspect ratio16:9
PlacementTop of the page, immediately after the introduction — this is the hero visual.
AccessibilityA horizontal journey diagram showing a TCP SYN packet travelling from a FortiGate to an ISP router, passing six labelled checkpoints, with the third checkpoint shown blocking it.
🧠
MEMORY ANCHOR
This is the image to recall when a session sits in Active. Walk the path mentally from left to right and stop at the first gate you have not personally verified — that gate is your next command.
VISUAL 02

Two Lies on the Ladder

Purpose: Fixes the single most confusing thing about the FSM: two of the six state names imply progress that has not happened. Isolating the two liars makes the other four trustworthy.

🧠
MEMORY ANCHOR
When you read a state name, ask whether the word is describing the protocol's intention or its achievement. 'Connect' and 'Active' describe intention. The other four describe achievement.
