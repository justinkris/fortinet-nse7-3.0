Title: The Journey — one SYN, six gates

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed illustrated vector artwork. Warm cream off-white background (#faf5e9). Coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines, deep navy ink for text. Clean bold line work. No gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D rendering, no lens flare. Generous spacing, professional but storybook-like, clear enough to function as an educational diagram. Recurring cast details must stay consistent: SENOR is an older engineer in a large decorative sombrero with a chaotic desk and immaculate diagrams behind him; JUNIOR is a young overconfident engineer, usually holding coffee; J.R. is Junior's identical twin but tidier, with a notebook and a clicking pen; PENNY is a business manager in a smart blazer holding a tablet; RILEY wears a help-desk headset; the INTERMEDIATE ENGINEER is competent and calm. The LoFinance logo is stacks of banknotes frozen inside a pale blue ice cube. COMPOSITION: a wide left-to-right journey across the full frame, like an illustrated board game path drawn as a winding road with clear stations. At the FAR LEFT, a stylised FortiGate appliance drawn as a friendly rounded box with a small face and status lights, labelled 'LoFinance HQ · AS 65001'. At the FAR RIGHT, a second appliance labelled 'ISP-1 Parramatta · AS 65100'. TRAVELLING ALONG THE ROAD: a single small character representing a TCP SYN packet — a rounded envelope with simple eyes and tiny legs, carrying a small suitcase labelled '179'. SIX GATES stand along the road, each drawn as a distinct illustrated barrier with a hand-lettered sign: GATE 1 'IS THERE A ROUTE?' drawn as a signpost with a folded map pinned to it; GATE 2 'RIGHT SOURCE ADDRESS?' drawn as a passport desk with a small stamp; GATE 3 'LOCAL-IN POLICY' drawn as a heavy closed portcullis in coral, clearly SHUT, with the SYN character bumping into it, one arm raised, expression startled — this gate must be visually the most prominent because it is the episode's actual fault; GATE 4 'TTL — CAN YOU SURVIVE ONE HOP?' drawn as a low tunnel with a height-limit bar and a small ticking hourglass; GATE 5 'UPSTREAM ACL' drawn as an open boom gate in sage green with a bored attendant; GATE 6 'DO THEY ANSWER?' drawn as a doorway with a bell. Beside GATE 3, a small inset speech bubble reads 'to the box, not through it'. A faint ghosted continuation of the road past the ISP router leads to a small banner reading 'ESTABLISHED' in muted gold, unreachable in this image. BOTTOM LEFT CORNER: the LoFinance logo. MUST NOT INCLUDE: configuration syntax, dense labels beyond those listed, photorealism, corporate stock art. LEARNING PURPOSE: convert an abstract list of failure causes into an ordered spatial walk, so the student checks causes in path order instead of at random.
