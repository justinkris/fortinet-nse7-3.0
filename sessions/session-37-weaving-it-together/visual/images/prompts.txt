Title: 1 · Root First — The Anchor and the Two Gates

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic yet highly detailed vector-art illustration, cream off-white background, coral and muted-gold accents, sage greens, soft brown outlines, clean bold line work, no gradients, no photorealism, storybook-professional style. A stylised castle-keep sits at the centre of a warm-toned enterprise map, its keystone labelled "ROOT" on a small printed label (label-maker aesthetic, slightly askew). A sombrero rests on the keep's flagpole. The keep has exactly ONE open gate on its inward-facing wall, glowing softly sage-green and marked "port5 — join here"; the outward wall facing a stormy coral sea labelled "ISP / INTERNET" is smooth stone with NO doors, a faint crowd of shadowy generic firewall silhouettes waiting hopelessly in the water. Inside the gate stands a second, smaller golden turnstile labelled "AUTHORIZE", operated by a calm security-officer character (Cipher: dark clothing, restrained posture, arms folded). Approaching the open gate along a tidy path: two confident FortiGate-shaped characters ("ISFW", "DCFW") and one rain-soaked, hopeful engineer holding a slightly wilted bouquet and a small firewall under his arm (Dave), finally being waved through. In the keep's tower window, a glowing archive book labelled "FAZ" — the keep visibly rests ON the book, showing the root requires FortiAnalyzer. Composition: slight elevated three-quarter view, keep centred, sea right, path left. Mood: orderly, safe, quietly triumphant. Must NOT include: real vendor logos, dense technical text, photorealistic people.

Prompt:
Flat detailed vector-art illustration, cream background, coral/gold/sage palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients. A cosy office break-room rendered as a cocktail party of anthropomorphised devices: a smart TV in a party hat chattering non-stop, a printer, laptops, a phone. Each guest unknowingly leaks identity, drawn as three distinct visible trails: (1) a small DNA double-helix name-tag pinned to each chest reading "MAC OUI — Samsung / Dell / HP"; (2) a speech bubble at each mouth showing a handshake introduction card "Hi, I'm Samsung-TV-Breakroom" tagged "DHCP"; (3) wavy coral accent-lines drifting from their conversations labelled subtly "DNS · SNI · User-Agent". At the doorway, low to the ground where guests ENTER, a small attentive FortiGate character (ISFW) sits in a listener's armchair with a notepad, ears comically large, writing everything down — clearly passive, holding no scanner or flashlight. Far in the background through a window, a distant larger firewall (NGFW) squints at blurry silhouettes, labelled "the edge sees shadows". Caption space at bottom for the line "Devices cannot shut up about what they are." Warm, humorous, memorable. Must NOT include: active-scanning imagery (radar beams from the FortiGate), vendor logos, photorealism.

Title: 4 · Spine and Memory — One Anchor, One Voucher

Prompt:
Flat detailed vector-art anatomical-metaphor illustration, cream background, coral/gold/sage, soft brown outlines, no gradients. The Security Fabric drawn as a friendly stylised tree-creature: the ROOT FortiGate is its SPINE and trunk (vertebrae subtly drawn in the bark, gold), branches ending in three smaller firewall characters (ISFW, DCFW, branch FGT). Two clearly separated flows: (1) sage-green envelope-shaped LOG leaves drifting from every branch outward to a large serene brain-shaped archive at the tree's side labelled "FAZ — MEMORY"; (2) a coral parcel labelled "suspicious file" travelling from a downstream branch UP THROUGH THE TRUNK/SPINE and out the top to a lab-coated analyst character with a magnifier labelled "FortiNDR", arrow path unmistakably through the trunk. At the tree's base, a small passport-control desk built INTO the trunk where Señor (sombrero) stamps ID booklets for admin characters, sign reading "IdP — ROOT ONLY · one fabric, one anchor, one voucher"; the branch firewalls each hold "SP" visitor badges, as do small FAZ and FMG figures queueing. Caption space: "Logs go to memory. Transit goes through the spine." Mood: calm, organic, didactic. Must NOT include: files travelling to the brain/FAZ, any second stamping desk, real logos, photorealism.
