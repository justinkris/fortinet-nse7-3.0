TECHNICAL CONCEPTS COVERED
--------------------------
1. The three jobs of any IKE handshake: (1) agree the language (proposals), (2) grow the
   shared secret (Diffie-Hellman), (3) prove identity (authentication).
2. THE ORDERING RULE: DH must complete BEFORE identities are sent, because pre-DH messages
   are necessarily plaintext. "First grow the secret, then whisper your name inside it."
3. IKEv1 aggressive mode = 3 msgs phase 1 + 3 quick mode = 6 total, peer IDs in CLEAR TEXT
   (sends identity before DH protection — that's WHY it's faster).
   IKEv1 main mode = 6 + 3 = 9 total, IDs encrypted.
   IKEv2 = IKE_SA_INIT (2 msgs: proposals + DH publics, plaintext but nothing sensitive)
   + IKE_AUTH (2 msgs: identities + auth, ENCRYPTED) = 4 total, IDs encrypted. No separate
   quick mode — first child SA piggybacks inside IKE_AUTH.
4. IKEv2 modern toolbox: AES-GCM, SHA-2/HMAC-SHA2 & AES-XCBC integrity, DH ECP groups 19/20
   (MODP-equivalent strength, less CPU), EAP support, integrated mode config, simplified
   renegotiation.
5. network-id: Fortinet-PROPRIETARY phase 1 attribute; selects the correct phase 1 between
   peers so MULTIPLE IKEv2 tunnels can exist between the same local/remote gateway pair.
   IKEv2 ONLY (IKEv1 = N/A). Must match at both ends.

MENTAL MODELS DEVELOPED
-----------------------
- "Judge a handshake by its job list and ORDER, not its message count" — the count is a
  consequence of the design, not the point.
- The secret is "GROWN, not posted" — DH key never crosses the wire because it never exists
  on the wire; it is born simultaneously at both ends from private+public halves.
- "First grow the secret, then whisper your name inside it" (Señor) — the ordering rule.
- NIGHTCLUB WITH TWO IDENTICAL DOORS — network-id ≈ the event printed on the ticket; the
  addresses can't disambiguate, the tag inside the negotiation does. Student's own phrase:
  "different event information" (arrived at it from one hint — log as near-self-generated).
- "Refuse the question" — when offered fast-vs-safe, first check whether the trade-off is
  real. (Reusable senior-engineer heuristic.)
