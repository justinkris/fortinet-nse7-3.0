Title: The Two Doctrines — One Map vs Many Treaties

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed illustrated vector artwork. Warm cream background (#faf5e9). Coral accents, muted gold highlights, sage greens, soft brown outlines, clean bold linework. No gradients, no photorealism, no glossy 3D rendering. Generous spacing. Professional but storybook-like, legible enough to function as an educational diagram. Split composition, two halves divided by a vertical seam. LEFT HALF: a warm domestic scene — four or five friendly router characters (simple rounded devices with small eyes and stubby antennae) sitting around a large kitchen table, ALL of them leaning over ONE enormous shared paper map spread across the table. Every router has an identical small copy of the same map in its hands. A cosy lamp, teacups, sage green tones. Label above in clean sans-serif: "OSPF — ONE MAP, ONE FAMILY". A small caption ribbon: "correctness from agreement". RIGHT HALF: a formal diplomatic scene — three router characters seated at separate negotiating desks, each behind a small flag on a stand, each holding its own DIFFERENT folded map held close to the chest, deliberately angled away from the others. Between them, sealed envelopes with wax stamps being passed by a courier. Coral and muted gold tones, more formal architecture with columns suggested by simple lines. Label above: "BGP — MANY MAPS, MANY TREATIES". Caption ribbon: "correctness from provenance". One of the diplomat routers wears a small sombrero. Another has a tiny Singapore flag. The seam between halves is a simple vertical line, not a dramatic crack. LoFinance logo (stacks of banknotes frozen inside a clear ice cube) small in the bottom-left corner. MUST NOT APPEAR: any technical labels like AS numbers or IP addresses, any packet or envelope icons on the left half, any hostility or conflict imagery — the right half is formal and polite, not adversarial.
