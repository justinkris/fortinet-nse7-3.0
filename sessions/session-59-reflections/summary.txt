TECHNICAL CONCEPTS COVERED
==================================================================
1. THE THREE RIBs (pp. 340-341) — the session's spine. The student ASKED FOR THIS EXPLICITLY
   ("Can you explain the RIB, I am getting confused"), which was the right call and was rewarded.
   - RIB-in    = everything I was told, raw, unprocessed, pre-filter. "Received."
   - Local RIB = what I chose to believe. Survived the IN filter, next hop resolves, won best-path.
   - RIB-out   = what I am willing to say to my peers. NOT automatically the same as Local RIB.
   Framing used: THREE DRAWERS, left to right, with a gate at each boundary.
2. TWO GATES ON THE RIB-in → Local RIB BOUNDARY, taught as a pair because the student named
   only one:
   - Gate 1: the IN filter (prefix-list-in, route-map-in) — "do I even want this?"
   - Gate 2: validity (is the NEXT_HOP resolvable?) + best-path selection.
   The student derived Gate 2 from the S58 fault scar, unprompted and correctly.
3. THE LOCAL RIB → RIB-out GATE AS THE HOME OF ALL POLICY. This was the session's biggest
   structural payoff and should be REUSED CONSTANTLY IN S60+. Everything taught across S58/S59
   lives at this one door: IBGP split horizon, RR relay rules, next-hop-self, prefix-list-out.
   All of them answer ONE question: "of the things I believe, what am I willing to say, and to whom?"
   Señor's frame: BELIEVING AND TELLING ARE DIFFERENT DRAWERS.
4. IBGP SPLIT HORIZON AS THE CAUSE OF FULL MESH. Retrieved cold (after one prompt) in exact
   wording: "a prefix learned from an IBGP peer is never advertised to ANY OTHER IBGP PEER."
   The student had to be walked to it once but produced it verbatim. Now OWNED.
5. ROUTE REFLECTORS AS A DELIBERATE RELAXATION OF SPLIT HORIZON — framed not as a new mechanism
   but as the removal of a safety rule. "The RR is breaking the split-horizon rule on purpose."
   An RR + its clients = a CLUSTER. Clients peer ONLY with the RR. The RR peers with other RRs
   and border routers. Mesh collapses from n(n-1)/2 to n.
6. THE CLIENT IS MUTE AND DOESN'T KNOW IT'S A CLIENT. Critical exam point, delivered twice.
   `set route-reflector-client enable` is configured ONLY ON THE RR, under the neighbour block
   for each client. Study guide wording: "The RR defines which neighbors are the clients."
   The client's config is an ORDINARY IBGP neighbour statement. Nothing on it says "client."
   Directional-trap teaching technique used: read the SHAPE of the command — everything inside
   `config neighbor / edit <ip>` describes THE PEER AT THAT ADDRESS. Compared explicitly to
   `set remote-as` and `set next-hop-self` to generalise the rule.
7. REPLACEMENT LOOP PREVENTION. Taught by ASKING BEFORE TELLING per the S58 handoff instruction,
   and the student PREDICTED IT (see performance).
   - ORIGINATOR_ID — router-ID of the client that originally injected the route. If a router sees
     its own router-ID here, it drops the route SILENTLY. Router-scope.
   - CLUSTER_LIST — cluster-ID prepended by each reflecting RR. If an RR sees its own cluster-ID,
     it drops the route. Cluster-scope. THIS IS THE INTER-CLUSTER / MULTI-RR GUARD.
   Presented as three NESTED RINGS, all asking the same question at different scales:
     Between ASes    → AS_PATH      → "is my AS in it?"
     Between clusters→ CLUSTER_LIST → "is my cluster-ID in it?"
     Within a cluster→ ORIGINATOR_ID→ "is my router-ID it?"
   "Same question at every scale: did this come from me?"
8. THE THREE RR RELAY CASES. Student over-tightened here twice; needed correcting.
   - From a CLIENT → reflect to EVERYONE (other clients, non-client IBGP peers, EBGP peers).
     Justification the student must hold: the client can't tell anyone itself, so if the RR
     doesn't relay, NOBODY hears it.
   - From a NON-CLIENT IBGP peer → reflect TO CLIENTS ONLY. Not to other non-clients (those are
     ordinary IBGP peers; original split horizon still binds).
   - From an EBGP peer → advertise to everyone. Split horizon never applied.
   COMPRESSED RULE GIVEN TO THE STUDENT (reuse this exact line):
     "Downward (to clients): always. Sideways (non-client to non-client): never."
   Señor's version: "The reflector speaks freely to its own. To strangers, it only repeats what
   its own told it."
9. THE OPERATIONAL PAYOFF, stated in Penny's language: adding Adelaide = one neighbour block on
   HQ with route-reflector-client enable, plus one ordinary neighbour block on Adelaide.
   TWO ROUTERS TOUCHED, NOT NINE. Eleven hours becomes twenty minutes.

MENTAL MODELS DEVELOPED
==================================================================
- THREE DRAWERS: received / believed / willing to say. This is now the primary BGP mental model
  and should anchor all of S60's attribute and best-path teaching.
- "Believing and telling are different drawers. Almost all of BGP policy is the door between them."
- EVERY SAFETY RULE HAS A PRICE, AND THE PRICE IS USUALLY SCALE. Split horizon buys structural
  loop-freedom and charges n(n-1)/2. Engineering is choosing which bill to pay.
- WHEN YOU RELAX A STRUCTURAL GUARANTEE, YOU MUST REPLACE IT WITH A DETECTION MECHANISM.
  The student predicted this unprompted — flag it as a transferable senior principle.
- Loops made IMPOSSIBLE (split horizon) vs loops DETECTED (ORIGINATOR_ID/CLUSTER_LIST). The RR
  design deliberately downgrades from the first to the second in exchange for scale.
- "THE MESH COLLAPSES BECAUSE ONLY THE CENTRE IS ALLOWED TO SPEAK. The clients are deliberately,
  permanently mute. That muteness is what makes the star safe."
- NAME THE DRAWER BEFORE YOU DIAGNOSE — introduced this session as the student's prescribed
  corrective habit (see growth area). "The route is in the Local RIB, so this is a RIB-out question."

TROUBLESHOOTING APPROACH (as taught this session)
==================================================================
RR-specific fault ladder:
1. Client has no routes from its peers        → is route-reflector-client set ON THE RR under
                                                 that client's neighbour block? Nothing on the
                                                 client will ever tell you. Check the RR.
2. Session Established, prefixes at RR, none  → check RIB-OUT on the RR, not RIB-in on the client.
   at client                                    (get router info bgp neighbors <client> advertised-routes)
3. Routes vanish silently at a client         → ORIGINATOR_ID matches the client's router-ID.
                                                 SILENT DROP, NO LOG. Same signature as S58:
                                                 absence of errors is not evidence of correctness.
4. Two RRs, oscillation or missing inter-      → CLUSTER_LIST / cluster-ID mismatch.
   cluster routes
5. Reflection working, traffic still failing  → wrong drawer. Back to next-hop resolution.
                                                 next-hop-self on the RR is almost always required.
