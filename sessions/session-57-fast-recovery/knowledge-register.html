GROWTH AREAS
==================================================================
1. **MODEL REAPPLICATION — THE PRINCIPAL PATTERN THIS SESSION.**
   He built the CPU-cost argument himself ("BGP is run by the CPU, it could get resource
   intensive") and then, twenty minutes later, when asked whether BFD across fifty peerings
   was wise, said it "would be good." The knowledge was present; the RETRIEVAL was not
   triggered by a structurally identical situation wearing different clothes.
   THIS IS NOT A KNOWLEDGE GAP. It is a transfer gap, and it is the most valuable thing to
   work on, because it is exactly what the exam tests: the same principle dressed differently.
   COACHING MOVE FOR S58-60: when he builds a model, NAME IT ALOUD as a portable object ("that
   is your CPU-cost argument — keep it") and then deliberately re-present the same constraint
   later in a new costume and see whether he reaches for it. Do this at least once per session.
2. **STATING THE CONTRAST, NOT JUST THE ANSWER.** The loopback/local-in cold pass produced the
   correct fix without the discriminating principle. He knows what is true; he does not always
   volunteer what is DIFFERENT. Exam distractors live entirely in that gap.
   COACHING MOVE: ask "why is the OTHER plausible answer wrong here?" as a routine follow-up.
3. **TERMINOLOGY PRECISION UNDER FLUENCY.** "Hello" for "keepalive." Happens when he is
   reasoning fast and the mechanism is clear — he reaches for the nearest adjacent term. Low
   priority for engineering, non-trivial for the exam. Correct in passing, do not dwell.
4. Money-sentence skill went unexercised. Not a regression, but do not let it lapse further.
