CENTRE: a simple stylised car seat viewed from the side, drawn in muted blue, with a single abstract geometric passenger shape seated in it — no facial features, no realistic human detail. The FIRST belt, drawn in sage green and labelled with a small tag reading 'GRACEFUL RESTART · hold, he is only restarting', runs diagonally and looks protective and intact at its anchor points. The SECOND belt, drawn in coral and labelled 'BFD · he is dead, reroute now', crosses the first at a sharp angle — and at the crossing point the coral belt is visibly SLICING THROUGH the sage belt, with the sage belt's severed end curling loose and falling away. ABOVE the seat, a small stopwatch in coral reading '750 ms' with a short arrow pointing down at the coral belt, indicating it acts first. TO THE RIGHT, a small routing-table icon showing three horizontal route lines with all three struck through in coral, labelled 'routes withdrawn anyway'. ACROSS THE BOTTOM, an elegant serif caption: 'You bought the seatbelt. Then you bought a second seatbelt that cuts the first one.' Colour discipline: only sage green, coral, muted blue and gold on cream. Do NOT include realistic humans, faces, vehicle interiors in detail, vendor logos, or CLI text. Purpose: make it impossible to forget that BFD overrules graceful restart when both are aggressive on the same neighbour.
