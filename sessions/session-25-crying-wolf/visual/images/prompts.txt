Title: The sheepdog and the hidden wolf

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic but highly detailed vector illustration, cream off-white background (#faf5e9), coral / muted-gold / sage-green / navy palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism. Title: Two postal clerks, two depths

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic highly-detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), navy / muted-gold / sage / coral palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism. A side-by-side diptych of the same sealed envelope handled two ways. LEFT panel, labelled "Certificate inspection": a postal clerk reads only the OUTSIDE of a sealed wax-stamped envelope — squinting at the address, the return sender, and the postmark, which are labelled in small tidy tags "SNI", "certificate name", "issuer". The wax seal stays intact. A small caption: "reads the envelope." RIGHT panel, labelled "Full (deep) inspection": the same envelope now OPEN, the wax seal broken, the clerk reading the unfolded LETTER inside — the letter's text is stylised as a line of code with one line highlighted in coral reading system('dir'); a tiny magnifying glass labelled "IPS" hovers over that line. Caption: "reads the letter." Between the two panels, a subtle key icon labelled "FortiGate holds the key" bridging them, signalling that full inspection requires the decryption key. Small frozen-cash-in-ice LoFinance mark in a corner. Clean symmetrical two-panel layout, bold linework, generous spacing, storybook-professional. Do NOT include: photorealistic paper, glossy 3D, dense text. Educational purpose: anchor that DEPTH is decided by whether the seal comes off — certificate never opens it, full does, and only full lets the IPS read the payload.

Title: The scanner on the conveyor belt

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic highly-detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), navy / sage / coral / muted-gold palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism. A left-to-right packet journey along a conveyor belt, three labelled stages. STAGE 1 (left): a sealed red-wax envelope enters from a client workstation icon, tagged "encrypted HTTPS". STAGE 2 (centre), inside a dashed rounded box labelled "FortiGate — flow mode": the envelope passes UNDER a coral scanning archway (like an airport scanner) — as it passes, the wax seal is shown lifting off in-stream and a thin coral beam reads the exposed letter WITHOUT stopping the belt; a small side note reads "sliding buffer — matches the pattern as it streams, never holds the whole file"; the seal is shown re-closing on the far side of the arch ("re-encrypt"). STAGE 3 (right): the envelope continues to a server icon labelled "trading server". BELOW the belt, a contrasting greyed-out inset panel labelled "Antivirus = proxy" shows a DIFFERENT lane where whole parcels are stacked and fully assembled in a holding bin before a stamp labelled "hash / unpack" — captioned "needs the whole file first." Motion cues: subtle speed lines on the flow belt, stillness in the proxy bin. Small LoFinance frozen-cash-in-ice mark in a corner. Clean horizontal composition, clear stage separation, generous spacing, storybook-professional. Do NOT include: glossy 3D, dense text, realistic machinery clutter. Educational purpose: flow reads a pattern mid-stream (low latency, feed-safe); proxy buffers the whole object (forced by reassembly, e.g. AV).

Title: The two-dial control panel

Prompt:
Flat minimalistic highly-detailed vector illustration, cream background (#faf5e9), navy / muted-gold / sage / coral palette, soft brown outlines, no gradients, no photorealism. A clean engineering control panel with TWO clearly separate labelled dials mounted side by side, deliberately NOT connected to each other. LEFT dial, labelled "DEPTH — what can the IPS read?": a two-position selector pointing between "Certificate (envelope)" and "Full (letter)", with a small sub-caption "set by ENCRYPTION". RIGHT dial, labelled "MODE — buffer the whole object?": a two-position selector between "Flow (stream)" and "Proxy (reassemble)", with a small sub-caption "set by REASSEMBLY". Between the two dials, a bold NOT-EQUALS or a small broken chain-link icon makes clear they are independent — plus a small note "full inspection works in BOTH modes". A tidy tag beneath reads "encryption ≠ reassembly". A calm mentor hand in frame adjusts the left dial only. Small LoFinance frozen-cash-in-ice mark in a corner. Clean balanced composition, strong separation between the two dials, generous whitespace, storybook-professional. Do NOT include: glossy 3D, tangled wiring, dense text. Educational purpose: keep the two axes separate — the single most common conceptual error on this topic.
