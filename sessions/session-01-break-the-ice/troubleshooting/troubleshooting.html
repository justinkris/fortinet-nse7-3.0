SESSION 01 · TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

Path → Policy → Feed

How a senior engineer investigates "the firewall didn't catch it" — before touching a single knob.

THE SCENARIO CLASS

Symptom: "Something bad happened and there are no logs"

Riley's version: "The traders got a virus and nobody warned us." Junior's version: "But the firewall is configured!" Session 1 taught exactly one troubleshooting discipline, and it is the most valuable one in the entire course: most detection failures are not feature failures. They are failures of path (traffic never crossed an inspection point) or feed (the engine's intelligence is stale). Only the middle case is a configuration problem.

The senior habit: establish the packet's path before forming any other hypothesis. If you can't state, out loud, every device the traffic traversed, you are not troubleshooting yet — you are guessing.
QUESTIONS TO ASK FIRST

Interrogate the Situation, Not the Box

  1. What was the traffic path? Source, destination, and every hop between. Did it ever traverse a FortiGate? (Workstation → switch → server = the firewall was never in the conversation.)
  2. Was there an inspection point with a security profile applied on the specific policy that matched this traffic? A profile that exists but isn't applied to the matching policy protects nothing.
  3. Was the traffic encrypted, and was SSL inspection in play? Engines without decryption inspect a locked box.
  4. Is the intelligence current? Licence valid, FortiGuard reachable, packages updating?
  5. What does "nothing was caught" actually mean? No logs at all vs logged-and-allowed — these point to completely different layers.
DECISION TREE

The Three-Verdict Tree

START: A threat got through / activity went undetected.
Q1 — Did the traffic traverse a FortiGate at all?
NO → PATH Architectural gap. No feature can fix a missing inspection point. Fix: segmentation — put the gateway on the firewall so the flow is forced through inspection.
YES ↓
Q2 — Did the matching firewall policy have the relevant security profile applied?
NO → POLICY Configuration gap. Apply the profile to the policy the traffic actually matched (not the policy you assumed it matched).
YES ↓
Q3 — Was the traffic encrypted without SSL inspection?
YES → POLICY The engines saw a sealed envelope. Full inspection required for content verdicts.
NO ↓
Q4 — Are FortiGuard licences valid, servers reachable, packages current?
NO → FEED Config ≠ protection. Restore the intelligence supply chain.
YES → Genuinely novel threat (zero-day) → this is what advanced threat protection / sandboxing exists for; escalate and capture evidence for TAC.

LIKELY ROOT CAUSES & EXAM TRAPS

Pattern-Match Like a Senior

  • No logs at all → traffic never reached the firewall. Almost always a path/architecture answer on the exam (lateral movement scenarios, USB-borne malware).
  • Logged and allowed → policy matched but no profile / wrong profile / no SSL inspection.
  • Was caught last month, missed today → feed problem: expired contract, unreachable FortiGuard, failed updates.
  • Exam trap wording: "signatures out of date" is only correct when the stem says detection degraded — never when the stem says the firewall saw nothing.
  • Exam trap wording: any answer where an NGFW "replaces" traditional firewalling is wrong on sight.
Say it like Señor: "Before you ask what the firewall thought of the packet, prove the firewall ever met the packet."
